# Investor Financing Report — Promotion & Outreach Kit

Companion to [`investor-financing-report.md`](./investor-financing-report.md). This is the
execution playbook for earning links and citations to the report. All numbers below are
pulled directly from the published 2024 dataset (`src/data/hmda-investor/`) — do not invent
or round-trip new figures; quote these.

**Strategic premise:** we do not have a large list or audience to "blast." Our leverage is
the *data itself* — a free, public-domain, 50-state dataset with a baked-in attribution
watermark. The play is to put the right number in front of the right writer at the moment
they need it. Every tactic below is data-led, not list-led.

## 2. National headline hooks (the one-liners)

Ready-to-quote facts. These are the "lede" for any pitch.

- Real estate investors paid an **88 bps rate premium** over owner-occupants in 2024 — a
  median **7.50%** vs **6.625%**.
- Investor applications were **denied 17.2% of the time** — far above the owner-occupant rate.
- **9.4%** of all single-family (1–4 unit) originations went to investors —
  **450,248 loans** worth **$142.3 billion**.
- Nearly **7 in 10** investor loans (69%) were flagged primarily business/commercial purpose —
  the bucket that captures **DSCR and LLC-held** lending.
- The typical investor put **~25% down** (75% median LTV); **17%** were cash-out refinances.

## 4. Target outlets & angle fit

| Outlet | Type | Best angle to lead with |
|---|---|---|
| HousingWire | Mortgage/housing trade | National premium + denial trend; DSCR/business-purpose growth |
| National Mortgage Professional | Mortgage trade | Loan-officer framing: where investor business is hardest/easiest to fund |
| REI INK | REI investor media | Investor-market-share + cash-out by state |
| BiggerPockets (blog + forums) | Investor community | State-by-state "what financing costs in your market" |
| The Mortgage Note / MPA | Housing news | Approval gap; business-purpose share |
| Local/regional business journals (ABJ network) | Local press | The single most extreme stat for *their* state (use §3 tables) |
| State & metro REIA chapter newsletters | Community | "Your market's numbers" + the state page link |
| Scotsman Guide | Lending trade | DSCR/non-QM angle (business-purpose share) |
| Inman | Real estate | Investor share of market; where investors are most active |

**Local press is the volume play.** There are 50 state pages and dozens of regional business
journals. A one-stat, one-link pitch tailored from §3 is low-effort and high-hit-rate because
you're handing them a local number they don't have.

## 5. Distribution channels (beyond cold pitching)

1. **Journalist-request platforms** — Qwoted, Help a B2B Writer, Featured (formerly Terkel),
   and HARO successors. Filter for housing/mortgage/real-estate queries; respond with the
   relevant stat + report link. Highest-ROI channel for a small brand: reporters come to *you*.
2. **BiggerPockets forums** — answer "what are DSCR rates / how hard is it to get an investor
   loan in [state]" threads with the state number + link. Be genuinely useful, not promotional.
3. **Reddit** — r/realestateinvesting, r/DSCR, market-specific subs. Same rule: lead with the
   data, link as the source.
4. **LinkedIn** — Bill posts a chart (watermarked) per week with one state's hook; tag the
   relevant trade context. The image carries attribution even when reshared.
5. **Mortgage/REI newsletter swaps** — offer the dataset as a "free data drop" to niche
   newsletters; they get content, we get the link.
6. **Wikipedia / data aggregators** — where editorially appropriate, the CSV + methodology make
   the report a citable secondary source for HMDA-derived investor stats.

## 6. Outreach templates

Keep these tight — one job per message, lead with the number, link once. (Bill's voice: short,
no fluff, no hard close.)

### A. Trade-press pitch (HousingWire / NMP / Scotsman)
> **Subject: Investors paid an 88 bps rate penalty in 2024 — full 50-state data**
>
> Hi [name] — we pulled the 2024 HMDA loan-level file and isolated investment-property lending
> across all 50 states. A few findings your readers would want:
> - Investors paid a median 88 bps over owner-occupants (7.50% vs 6.625%), and were denied 17.2% of the time.
> - 69% of investor loans were business-purpose — the DSCR/LLC bucket.
> - Mississippi has the steepest premium (163 bps); Utah the lowest (50).
>
> Full report + downloadable dataset here: [link]. Happy to share the raw CSV or pull a specific cut.

### B. Local business journal (per-state)
> **Subject: [STATE] investors pay [X] bps more to finance a rental — 2024 data**
>

> premium over owner-occupants and faced a [Y]% denial rate, [rank context]. Local page with the
> numbers and a chart you're welcome to use: [state link]. Raw data available if useful.

### C. Journalist-request platform response (Qwoted/Featured)
> Per your query on [topic]: in 2024, [the one stat that answers their question, from §2/§3].
> Source is our 50-state HMDA investor-financing analysis: [link]. Full dataset (CSV) and

## 8. Sequencing (a realistic rollout for a small brand)

1. **Week 1 — foundation.** Submit the dataset to Google Dataset Search (Dataset JSON-LD is
   already live). Set up Qwoted/Featured/Help-a-B2B-Writer monitoring for housing queries.
2. **Week 1–2 — trade.** Pitch HousingWire, NMP, Scotsman, REI INK with the national hook (Template A).
3. **Week 2–4 — local drip.** 3–5 per-state pitches/week to regional business journals + REIA
   chapters using §3 + Template B. Prioritize the extreme states (MS, DC, UT, NY, HI).
4. **Ongoing — community + social.** Weekly watermarked-chart LinkedIn post; answer forum/Reddit
   threads as they surface. Respond to every relevant journalist request same-day.
5. **Annual — refresh.** When the next HMDA vintage drops, re-run the pipeline, publish the new
   year, and pitch the *year-over-year change* as a fresh hook.

## 9. Measuring what works

- **Referring domains** — track new links via Ahrefs Site Explorer on `/reports/investor-financing*`.
- **GSC** — watch impressions/clicks on the report + state pages; new linking domains show up as
  referral traffic in GA4.
- **Citations without links** — periodically search the headline stats ("88 bps rate premium
  investors HMDA") to catch uncredited pickups worth a polite link request.
- **What to double down on:** whichever channel produces the first 2–3 referring domains gets the
  next month's focus. Don't spread thin.
