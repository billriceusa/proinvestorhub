# Investor Financing Report — Promotion & Outreach Kit

Companion to [`investor-financing-report.md`](./investor-financing-report.md). This is the
execution playbook for earning links and citations to the report. All numbers below are
pulled directly from the published 2025 dataset (`src/data/hmda-investor/`) and the
2024→2025 trend layer (`trends.json`) — do not invent or round-trip new figures; quote these.

**Strategic premise:** we do not have a large list or audience to "blast." Our leverage is
the *data itself* — a free, public-domain, 50-state dataset with a baked-in attribution
watermark, now with a year-over-year trend line. The play is to put the right number in
front of the right writer at the moment they need it. Every tactic below is data-led, not
list-led.

**What's new in 2025:** the report now carries a full year-over-year comparison. The
*change* is the freshest, most pitchable hook — reporters cover movement, not levels.

## 2. National headline hooks (the one-liners)

Ready-to-quote facts. The **year-over-year** lines are the strongest — lead with movement.

**The trend (lead with these):**

- The real estate investor **rate premium narrowed to 88→76 bps** in 2025 — and it
  **compressed in 42 states, held flat in 9, and widened in zero.**
- Investors borrowed **16% more loans** (450,248 → 524,314) and **28% more dollars**
  ($142.3B → $181.6B) even as rates fell.
- **Cash-out refinancing jumped** — 17.0% → 20.5% of investor loans (+3.5 points) as
  investors tapped equity into a falling-rate market.
- **DSCR / business-purpose lending kept professionalizing** — 69% → 72% of investor loans.

**The levels (2025):**

- Investors paid a **76 bps premium** over owner-occupants — a median **7.25%** vs **6.49%**.
- Investor applications were **denied 17.5% of the time** — far above the owner-occupant rate.
- **9.8%** of all single-family (1–4 unit) originations went to investors —
  **524,314 loans** worth **$181.6 billion**.
- Nearly **3 in 4** investor loans (72%) were flagged primarily business/commercial purpose —
  the bucket that captures **DSCR and LLC-held** lending.
- The typical investor put **~26% down** (74% median LTV); **21%** were cash-out refinances.

| Outlet | Type | Best angle to lead with |
|---|---|---|
| HousingWire | Mortgage/housing trade | YoY premium compression; DSCR/business-purpose growth |
| National Mortgage Professional | Mortgage trade | Loan-officer framing: where investor business is hardest/easiest to fund |
| REI INK | REI investor media | Investor-market-share + cash-out surge by state |

| The Mortgage Note / MPA | Housing news | Approval gap; business-purpose share; YoY originations jump |
| Local/regional business journals (ABJ network) | Local press | The single most extreme stat (or biggest move) for *their* state (use §3) |
| State & metro REIA chapter newsletters | Community | "Your market's numbers, then vs now" + the state page link |
| Scotsman Guide | Lending trade | DSCR/non-QM angle (business-purpose share) |
| Inman | Real estate | Investor share of market + the cash-out refinancing surge |

**Local press is the volume play.** There are 50 state pages and dozens of regional business
journals. A one-stat, one-link pitch tailored from §3 — especially the *change* — is
low-effort and high-hit-rate because you're handing them a local number they don't have.

## 5. Distribution channels (beyond cold pitching)

1. **Journalist-request platforms** — Qwoted, Help a B2B Writer, Featured (formerly Terkel),
   and HARO successors. Filter for housing/mortgage/real-estate queries; respond with the
   relevant stat + report link. Highest-ROI channel for a small brand: reporters come to *you*.
2. **BiggerPockets forums** — answer "what are DSCR rates / how hard is it to get an investor
   loan in [state]" threads with the state number + link. Be genuinely useful, not promotional.
3. **Reddit** — r/realestateinvesting, r/DSCR, market-specific subs. Same rule: lead with the
   data, link as the source.
4. **LinkedIn** — Bill posts a chart (watermarked) per week with one state's hook or YoY move;
   the image carries attribution even when reshared.
5. **Mortgage/REI newsletter swaps** — offer the dataset as a "free data drop" to niche
   newsletters; they get content, we get the link.
6. **Wikipedia / data aggregators** — where editorially appropriate, the CSV + methodology make
   the report a citable secondary source for HMDA-derived investor stats.

## 6. Outreach templates

Keep these tight — one job per message, lead with the number, link once. (Bill's voice: short,
no fluff, no hard close.)

## 8. Sequencing (a realistic rollout for a small brand)

1. **Week 1 — foundation.** Submit the dataset to Google Dataset Search (Dataset JSON-LD is
   already live). Set up Qwoted/Featured/Help-a-B2B-Writer monitoring for housing queries.
2. **Week 1–2 — trade.** Pitch HousingWire, NMP, Scotsman, REI INK with the YoY hook (Template A).
3. **Week 2–4 — local drip.** 3–5 per-state pitches/week to regional business journals + REIA
   chapters using §3 + Template B. Prioritize the biggest movers (MS, AR, NE, LA) and the
   extreme states (AK, DC, VT, NY, HI).
4. **Ongoing — community + social.** Weekly watermarked-chart LinkedIn post; answer forum/Reddit
   threads as they surface. Respond to every relevant journalist request same-day.
5. **Mid-year — Snapshot reconcile.** When the 2025 Snapshot National Loan-Level Dataset
   publishes (~July), re-run the pipeline to finalize the figures and note any revisions.
6. **Annual — refresh.** When the next HMDA vintage drops, re-run the pipeline, publish the new
   year, and pitch the *year-over-year change* as a fresh hook (now built in).

## 9. Measuring what works

- **Referring domains** — track new links via Ahrefs Site Explorer on `/reports/investor-financing*`.
- **GSC** — watch impressions/clicks on the report + state pages; new linking domains show up as
  referral traffic in GA4.
- **Citations without links** — periodically search the headline stats ("investor rate premium
  compressed HMDA 2025") to catch uncredited pickups worth a polite link request.
- **What to double down on:** whichever channel produces the first 2–3 referring domains gets the
  next month's focus. Don't spread thin.
