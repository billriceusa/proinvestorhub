# DSCR Loan Requirements: The Investor's Complete 2026 Checklist

To qualify for a DSCR loan in 2026, you need a minimum credit score of 620–680 (varies by lender), a 20–25% down payment, 3–6 months of cash reserves, and a property DSCR ratio of at least 1.0 — meaning gross monthly rent must equal or exceed the full mortgage payment (principal, interest, taxes, insurance, and HOA). Unlike conventional loans, there are no W-2s, tax returns, or personal debt-to-income calculations. The property's income qualifies you, not your paycheck.

---

## What Is a DSCR Loan and Why It Matters for Investors

A DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) loan is a non-QM (non-qualified mortgage) investment property loan that underwrites based solely on whether the rental property generates enough income to cover its debt payments. The formula is simple:

**DSCR = Gross Monthly Rent ÷ Monthly PITIA**

*(PITIA = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + HOA, if applicable)*

A DSCR of 1.0 means rents exactly cover the mortgage. A 1.25 DSCR means rents are 25% higher than the payment — that's the sweet spot most lenders want to see.

DSCR loans are the dominant financing product for buy-and-hold investors scaling past their first few properties. When your W-2 income or debt-to-income ratio becomes a bottleneck, DSCR loans remove that ceiling entirely. You can close in an LLC, hold unlimited properties, and use projected market rents if the property is vacant at purchase.

## The 6 Core DSCR Loan Requirements

### 1. DSCR Ratio: The Property Must Cash-Flow

Most lenders require a **minimum DSCR of 1.0**, though requirements vary:

| DSCR Tier | Lender Interpretation | Impact on Terms |
|---|---|---|
| Below 0.75 | Ineligible at most lenders | — |
| 0.75–0.99 | Some lenders allow with rate premium | Higher rate, lower LTV |
| 1.0–1.09 | Standard qualifying floor | Base rate, standard LTV |
| 1.10–1.24 | Strong approval | Better pricing possible |
| 1.25+ | Premium borrower profile | Best rates and terms |

**Worked example:**

A single-family rental in Columbus, Ohio has market rent of $2,200/month. You're purchasing at $280,000 with 20% down ($56,000). At a 7.5% rate on a 30-year fixed, your PITIA (including estimated taxes and insurance) comes to approximately $1,870/month.

DSCR = $2,200 ÷ $1,870 = **1.18** → Qualifies at standard terms.

If market rent were $1,800/month instead, DSCR = 0.96 — you'd need to find a lender with a sub-1.0 program or increase your down payment to lower the monthly payment.

### 2. Credit Score: 620 Is the Floor, 720+ Gets You the Best Rates

Unlike conventional loans that typically require 740+ for investment properties, DSCR loans are accessible across a wider credit range:

| Credit Score Range | Typical DSCR Access | Rate Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 620–639 | Narrow lender set (RCN Capital, some portfolio lenders) | +1.0–1.5% rate premium |
| 640–679 | Most DSCR lenders (Kiavi, Lima One) | +0.5–1.0% premium |
| 680–719 | Full lender access, competitive pricing | Near market rate |
| 720+ | Best available rates and terms, no overlays | Best rate tier |

Key distinction: the credit score requirement applies to the **guarantor** (the person signing for the loan), not to an LLC entity. Even if you hold the property in an LLC, a personal guaranty is typically required.

**How lenders pull credit:** Most use a tri-merge (all three bureaus) and take the middle score. If you have a co-borrower, they typically take the lower of the two middle scores.

### 3. Down Payment: 20–25% Is Standard

The standard down payment for DSCR loans is **20–25%** of the purchase price:

- **Purchase loans:** 20–25% down (75–80% LTV)
- **Cash-out refinances:** Maximum 70–75% LTV (25–30% equity required)
- **Rate-and-term refinances:** Up to 80% LTV

**Can you use gifted funds or a cash-out refi from another property?** Yes, on most DSCR programs. Many investors cycle through a cash-out refinance on an existing property to fund the down payment on the next acquisition — a core component of the [BRRRR strategy](/calculators/brrrr).

You cannot use a simultaneous second lien (seller financing, HELOC from a different property paid at closing) as your down payment unless disclosed upfront. Funds must be seasoned at least 60–90 days in most cases.

### 4. Cash Reserves: 3–12 Months of PITIA

This is the requirement most investors underestimate. After your down payment and closing costs, you need liquid reserves:

- **Minimum:** 3 months PITIA on the subject property
- **Typical:** 6 months for most approvals
- **For multiple properties:** Some lenders require reserves on all financed investment properties in your portfolio, not just the new one

**What counts as reserves:**
- Checking and savings accounts
- Brokerage accounts (stocks, ETFs — typically 70% of current value)
- 401(k)/IRA funds (typically 60–70% of vested balance)
- Proceeds from a cash-out refinance at closing

**What does NOT count:** Equity you haven't yet accessed, anticipated rental income, the down payment funds after use.

*For a $300,000 loan with $2,000/month PITIA, 6 months of reserves = $12,000 in liquid assets after closing.*

### 5. Property Type: What's Eligible

DSCR loans cover a wider property range than conventional investment mortgages:

**Typically eligible:**
- Single-family residences (1–4 units) — most common
- Condominiums (warrantable and often non-warrantable)
- Townhouses
- Short-term rentals (with STR-supporting lenders)
- Rural properties on acreage (lender dependent)

**Typically ineligible or requires specialty programs:**
- 5+ unit multifamily (commercial/bridge loan territory)
- Primary residences or second homes
- Commercial properties (office, retail, industrial)
- Working farms or properties with significant agricultural use
- Properties with deferred maintenance or condition issues (major repairs needed)
- Mobile homes or manufactured homes (not on permanent foundation)

### 6. Loan Amount: $75K–$3M for Most Lenders

Most DSCR programs fall in a $75,000–$3,000,000 range per loan. A few key notes:

- **Minimum loan amounts:** $75,000 (Lima One, RCN) to $100,000 (Kiavi) — loans below minimums typically need portfolio lender or hard money alternatives
- **Jumbo DSCR:** Loans above $1.5M–$2M often have stricter overlays (higher credit score, lower LTV, larger reserves)
- **No property count cap:** Unlike conventional Fannie/Freddie loans (limited to 10 financed properties), DSCR has no limit on the number of properties you can hold

## Common Disqualifiers — and How to Fix Them

### "My DSCR is below 1.0"

**The fix:** Three levers move the DSCR:
1. **Increase down payment** — a larger down payment lowers the monthly payment, raising your DSCR
2. **Find a sub-1.0 lender** — some lenders (typically with rate penalties) allow DSCR down to 0.75
3. **Wait for the right market** — if local rents are rising, a deal that doesn't cash-flow today might in 12 months

### "My credit score is below 620"

**The fix:** DSCR programs below 620 are extremely limited. Hard money or private money lending may bridge the gap while you build credit. Focus on: paying down revolving balances below 30% utilization, disputing errors on your credit report, and avoiding new credit inquiries 6+ months before application.

### "I don't have enough for the down payment"

**The fix:** Legitimate options include: seller financing for a portion of the purchase price (disclosed upfront), partnering with an equity partner who provides the down payment in exchange for ownership, or executing a cash-out refinance on an existing property you've built equity in. [Run the numbers on your current equity first with our Rental Cash Flow Calculator](/calculators/rental-cashflow).

### "The property needs repairs"

**The fix:** DSCR lenders underwrite on current as-is value, not after-repair value. A property in poor condition either won't appraise at purchase price or will fail the lender's property condition requirements. In this case, a fix-and-flip loan or bridge loan is the right tool first — then refinance into a DSCR once the property is stabilized and rented.

## Special Situations: LLCs, BRRRR, and Short-Term Rentals

### Closing in an LLC

Most DSCR lenders allow you to close the loan in an LLC. This is a significant advantage over conventional investment property loans, which typically require personal ownership. LLC ownership provides liability protection, easier portfolio management, and potentially cleaner accounting.

Important: you will typically still need to sign a personal guaranty. The loan may also trigger a due-on-sale clause if you later transfer personally owned property into an LLC — consult a real estate attorney before doing so.

### DSCR for the BRRRR Strategy

DSCR is the natural exit refinance product for the BRRRR method (Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat). After the rehab is complete and the property is rented:

1. Use a bridge loan or hard money during acquisition and rehab
2. Once stabilized with a tenant in place, refinance into a 30-year DSCR loan
3. Pull out equity (cash-out DSCR refi) to fund the next acquisition

The DSCR must work based on post-stabilization rents and post-refi payment. Model this before you buy with our [BRRRR Calculator](/calculators/brrrr).

### Short-Term Rental DSCR Loans

Lenders that accept STR income typically use one of two methods:
- **AirDNA market data:** Estimated annual revenue ÷ 12 = monthly income for DSCR calculation
- **12-month rental history:** If you already operate the property as an STR, actual platform-reported income can be used

STR DSCR programs typically have higher minimum credit scores (680+) and lower LTV limits (70–75%) than standard long-term rental programs.

1. **Get your credit report clean before applying.** Dispute errors, pay down revolving balances, and avoid new credit inquiries for 90 days before submitting an application.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a lease in place to get a DSCR loan?**
No. Most lenders use an appraiser-determined market rent if the property is vacant. However, having an executed lease typically speeds up the process and can support a higher rent figure than the appraiser's estimate.

**Can I use a DSCR loan on my first investment property?**
Yes. Multiple lenders (Kiavi, RCN Capital, Lima One) explicitly allow first-time investors. You may face slightly stricter LTV requirements (75% vs 80%) at some lenders, but DSCR is increasingly accessible to beginners.

**What is the difference between DSCR and a conventional investment property loan?**
Conventional investment loans use your personal W-2 income, tax returns, and debt-to-income ratio. They cap you at 10 financed properties and offer lower rates. DSCR uses only the property's income and has no property count limit, but rates are typically 1–2% higher. Most investors start with conventional financing and switch to DSCR as their portfolio grows.

**Are DSCR loans available for multifamily properties?**
Yes, for 2–4 unit properties (residential). Five or more units requires commercial financing, typically through a different product class.

**How long does DSCR loan approval take?**
Most DSCR lenders can close in 14–21 days for experienced borrowers. First-time applications or complex property types may take 21–30 days. The appraisal (including rent schedule) is typically the longest-lead item.
