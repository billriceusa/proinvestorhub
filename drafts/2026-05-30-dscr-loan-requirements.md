# DSCR Loan Requirements (2026): What Every Real Estate Investor Must Know Before Applying

DSCR loans qualify you based on the rental income a property generates — not your W-2 or tax returns. In 2026, the core requirements are: a DSCR ratio of at least 1.00 (rental income ≥ debt service), a credit score of 620–680 depending on the lender, 20–25% down payment on a purchase, and an eligible investment property (SFR, 2–4 units, or short-term rentals in most programs). No personal income documentation required. That's the short version — here's everything you need to actually get approved.

---

## What Is a DSCR Loan?

A DSCR loan (Debt Service Coverage Ratio loan) is a non-QM investment property mortgage that underwrites based on the property's cash flow rather than your personal income. Lenders calculate whether a property's gross rental income is sufficient to cover its debt obligations — principal, interest, taxes, insurance, and HOA fees (PITIA).

This structure directly solves one of the biggest bottlenecks in scaling a rental portfolio: the Fannie Mae 10-loan conventional limit. Once you've exhausted conventional financing, DSCR loans let you keep acquiring without W-2 income constraints, DTI calculations, or employment verification.

The tradeoff: DSCR rates run roughly 0.5–1.5% higher than comparable conventional investment loans, and lenders typically require slightly larger down payments. [VERIFY: confirm rate premium vs. conventional in mid-2026 market]

## How the DSCR Formula Works

**DSCR = Gross Monthly Rent ÷ Monthly PITIA**

Where PITIA = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + HOA (if applicable)

**Example:**

- Purchase price: $320,000
- Down payment (25%): $80,000
- Loan amount: $240,000
- Interest rate: 7.25% (30-year fixed) [VERIFY: confirm representative rate at time of publication]
- Monthly P&I: ~$1,638
- Estimated taxes + insurance: ~$375/mo
- Monthly PITIA: **~$2,013**
- Market rent: **$2,400/mo**
- **DSCR = $2,400 ÷ $2,013 = 1.19** ✅ (qualifies with most lenders)

If rent dropped to $2,013, DSCR = 1.00 — the minimum threshold for most standard DSCR programs. At $1,800 rent, DSCR = 0.89, which puts you in "sub-1 DSCR" territory (some lenders allow this with compensating factors; most do not).

## Core DSCR Loan Requirements in 2026

### 1. Minimum DSCR Ratio

| DSCR Level | What It Means | Typical Lender Response |
|---|---|---|
| 1.25+ | Excellent cash flow | Easiest approval, best rates |
| 1.10–1.24 | Solid coverage | Standard approval |
| 1.00–1.09 | Breakeven coverage | Qualifies, tighter terms |
| 0.75–0.99 | Negative cash flow | Some lenders accept with 30%+ down and 720+ credit |
| Below 0.75 | Significant shortfall | Very few programs available |

Most lenders set the floor at **1.00**. A handful of programs (typically marketed as "no-ratio" or "sub-1 DSCR" loans) allow ratios below 1.00 if compensating factors — higher credit scores, significant cash reserves, lower LTV — offset the coverage gap. Expect meaningfully higher rates and fees in those programs.

### 2. Credit Score

The minimum credit score varies by lender and loan terms:

- **620**: The lowest floor you'll find, offered by programs like RCN Capital's DSCR product for investors with strong property fundamentals
- **640–660**: The most common standard minimum across DSCR lenders
- **680+**: Required by some programs for the best rates or for sub-1 DSCR loans
- **720+**: Unlocks the lowest rates and highest LTVs at most lenders

Your FICO score affects not just whether you qualify, but the rate you'll pay. A 720 score can save you 0.25–0.75% in rate versus a 660 on the same loan. [VERIFY: confirm rate tiering ranges at time of publication]

### 3. Down Payment and LTV

**Purchase:** 20–25% down is standard. Most lenders cap purchase LTV at 75–80%.

**Cash-out refinance:** Maximum LTV typically 70–75% (lenders take on more risk with cash-out).

**Rate-and-term refinance:** Up to 75–80% LTV in most programs.

Using a [DSCR cash-out refinance on an existing property to fund the down payment on a new acquisition](/calculators/rental-cashflow) is one of the most effective portfolio-scaling strategies. Run the numbers before committing — you need the refi to maintain positive DSCR at the new loan amount.

### 4. Property Type Eligibility

**Eligible:**
- Single-family residences (SFR) — the most common and straightforward
- 2–4 unit residential properties
- Short-term rentals (Airbnb, VRBO) — lenders use either market long-term rents or 12-month STR income history [VERIFY: confirm which method is more common in 2026]
- Condos (warrantable and non-warrantable — the latter requires specific programs)
- Rural properties (varies significantly by lender and geography)

**Generally ineligible:** 5+ unit commercial multifamily (needs commercial DSCR, different product), owner-occupied properties, raw land, vacation homes used by owner.

**LLC vs. personal name:** DSCR loans allow closing in your personal name or through an LLC. Many investors choose LLC ownership for liability separation and estate planning. Check with your attorney — some states require additional steps for LLC closings.

### 5. Loan Size and Other Parameters

- **Minimum loan amount:** $75,000–$100,000 (varies by lender)
- **Maximum loan amount:** $2M–$5M depending on lender (jumbo DSCR available in some programs)
- **Loan terms:** 15-year, 30-year, and 40-year fixed; interest-only options available (30- and 40-year IO)
- **Prepayment penalty:** Common — typically a 3-2-1 or 5-4-3-2-1 step-down. Negotiate for shorter penalties if your hold period may be under 5 years.
- **Foreign nationals:** Some lenders (notably RCN Capital) offer DSCR programs for non-U.S. citizens. Expect higher down payments and credit requirements.
- **No-ratio programs:** A small number of lenders offer DSCR loans with no rental income verification for borrowers with strong financial profiles [VERIFY: confirm availability of no-ratio DSCR in 2026 market]

## How to Improve Your DSCR Before Applying

This is the part most articles skip. If your DSCR is coming in at 0.90–0.99, here's how to move the needle before application rather than accepting worse terms.

**Increase the numerator (rental income):**
- Get a professional rent comps analysis — appraisers use market rents, so if your lease is below market, an appraisal may establish a higher income basis
- For STR properties, 12 months of documented Airbnb/VRBO income can replace or supplement market rent estimates
- Add an ADU or finish existing space to increase leasable square footage [VERIFY: confirm lender acceptance of ADU income in DSCR calculations]

**Decrease the denominator (PITIA):**
- Larger down payment = lower loan amount = lower monthly payment
- Buy down the interest rate with points — model this carefully using the [mortgage calculator](/calculators/mortgage)
- Shop for lower property tax assessments if you believe the property is over-assessed (long-term strategy)
- Reduce HOA exposure — some property types carry high HOA fees that compress DSCR significantly

**Adjust the deal structure:**
- Consider an interest-only loan term to lower monthly PITIA during the hold period. IO reduces monthly payment, improving DSCR, but doesn't build equity — ensure your exit strategy accounts for this.
- Negotiate the purchase price. Every $10,000 reduction in price cuts ~$60–70/mo in PITIA (at 7.25%), improving your DSCR ratio by ~2–3 points on a $2,000/mo rent property.

## DSCR Loans in the BRRRR Strategy

DSCR is the "R" in BRRRR — the refinance step. Here's how it flows:

1. **Buy** — Acquire distressed property with bridge or hard money
2. **Rehab** — Force appreciation through renovation
3. **Rent** — Place tenants; establish rental income basis
4. **Refinance** — Replace the short-term bridge loan with a long-term DSCR loan at the post-renovation value
5. **Repeat** — Use cash-out proceeds for the next deal

The DSCR refinance is where the BRRRR strategy realizes its capital efficiency. A $200,000 purchase, $40,000 renovation, and $300,000 ARV at 75% LTV DSCR cash-out = $225,000 refi — potentially recovering your initial capital and leaving you with a cash-flowing asset.

## DSCR vs. Conventional Investment Loans: When to Use Which

| Factor | DSCR Loan | Conventional (Fannie/Freddie) |
|---|---|---|
| Income verification | Property cash flow only | W-2, tax returns, DTI |
| Max properties | Unlimited | 10 total (Fannie Mae) |
| Rate (typical) | 7.0–9.0% | 6.25–7.5% [VERIFY] |
| LTV (purchase) | Up to 80% | Up to 80% |
| Underwriting speed | 14–21 days | 30–45 days |
| Self-employed investors | Yes, no income docs | Difficult (2 yr returns needed) |
| Portfolio scaling | Purpose-built | Caps out |

**Use conventional** when: you have a clean W-2 income profile, this is one of your first 1–4 investment properties, and the rate differential justifies the documentation burden.

**Use DSCR** when: you're scaling beyond 4–10 doors, your income is complex or non-W-2, you want to close faster, or you're acquiring through an LLC.

## What to Do If Your DSCR Is Below 1.0

A sub-1 DSCR does not automatically disqualify you — but it narrows your options significantly and increases your cost of capital.

**Options when DSCR < 1.0:**

1. **Sub-1 DSCR programs:** Some lenders accept DSCR as low as 0.75 with 30–35% down and 720+ credit. Rates are typically 0.5–1.0% higher than standard programs.
2. **Blanket loan:** If you have multiple properties with strong DSCR, some lenders will underwrite across the portfolio rather than each property individually.
3. **Hard money + lease-up:** Acquire with bridge financing, stabilize with a lease at market rent, then refinance into DSCR once income basis is established.
4. **Price renegotiation:** If the deal pencils at a lower price and the seller has flexibility, a 5–8% price reduction could push you from 0.95 to 1.05 DSCR — the difference between no and yes.
5. **Reassess the deal:** A persistent sub-1 DSCR isn't just a financing problem — it's a fundamentals problem. The property may not generate enough income to support the acquisition at this price.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do DSCR loans require income verification?**
No. DSCR loans do not require W-2s, pay stubs, tax returns, or employment verification. Qualification is based entirely on the property's rental income relative to debt service.

**Can I get a DSCR loan with a 600 credit score?**
It's difficult. Most programs start at 620, and sub-640 scores face very limited options with higher down payment requirements. Focus on improving your score to 640+ before applying.

**How long does it take to close a DSCR loan?**
Most lenders close in 14–21 days. Specialist DSCR shops like Kiavi can close in as few as 10 days for straightforward deals. Factor this into your contract timelines.

**Can I use projected rental income to qualify?**
Yes — if the property is vacant at the time of application, lenders typically accept the market rent established by the appraisal. An executed lease is not required in most programs.

**Is there a prepayment penalty?**
Most DSCR loans include prepayment penalties, typically structured as a 3-2-1 step-down (3% in year 1, 2% in year 2, 1% in year 3). Some lenders offer shorter windows. Negotiate this term if you have flexibility on rate.

**Can I put a DSCR loan in my LLC?**
Yes. DSCR loans allow you to take title in your personal name or through an LLC. Closing in an LLC is common for portfolio protection and estate planning purposes. Ask your lender about any LLC seasoning requirements.
