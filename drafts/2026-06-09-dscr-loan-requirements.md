# DSCR Loan Requirements: The Complete 2026 Investor's Guide

To qualify for a DSCR loan in 2026, you generally need: a minimum **620–680 credit score**, a **20–25% down payment**, a **DSCR ratio of 1.0 or higher** (rental income covers the full mortgage payment), a property appraisal that includes a rent schedule, and **3–6 months of cash reserves** after closing. Unlike conventional mortgages, DSCR loans do not require W-2s, pay stubs, or personal tax returns — the property's rental income is what qualifies the loan.

This guide explains every requirement in plain terms, shows how they shift based on your credit profile and investor situation, and helps you prepare a strong application.

---

## What Is a DSCR Loan?

A Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) loan is a real estate investment mortgage that qualifies borrowers on the property's income rather than the investor's personal income. The core question the lender asks is: *does this property generate enough rent to cover its own mortgage payment?*

That single shift in underwriting logic makes DSCR loans the go-to financing product for:

- **Portfolio investors** scaling past the 10-property conventional loan cap
- **Self-employed investors** whose tax returns show low net income due to depreciation write-offs
- **Full-time investors** who lack W-2 documentation
- **Short-term rental operators** (Airbnb, VRBO) who earn income differently than traditional landlords

## 1. The DSCR Ratio: The Most Important Requirement

The DSCR ratio is the centerpiece of every DSCR loan underwrite. It measures whether the property pays for itself.

**The formula:**

> **DSCR = Gross Monthly Rental Income ÷ Total Monthly Debt Service (PITIA)**

PITIA = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + HOA/Condo fees (if applicable)

**Example:**
- Monthly rent: $2,200
- Monthly PITIA: $1,760
- DSCR = 2,200 ÷ 1,760 = **1.25** ✓

### DSCR Ratio Tiers — What They Mean for Your Loan

| DSCR | Status | Typical Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| **1.25+** | Strong cash flow | Best rates, 80% LTV available, all programs open |
| **1.0–1.24** | Break-even to moderate | Standard approval, competitive rates |
| **0.75–0.99** | Below break-even | "No-ratio" programs only; 30–35% down, rate premium |
| **Below 0.75** | Significant shortfall | Most lenders decline |

A minority of lenders offer "no-ratio" DSCR programs for properties with high appreciation potential or minor-rehab upside, but the rate premium is meaningful [VERIFY: confirm current no-ratio rate premium range with individual lenders before quoting clients].

## 2. Credit Score Requirements

DSCR loans skip income verification, but they still scrutinize your credit history carefully. Your credit score determines three things: whether you get approved, what rate you'll pay, and how much LTV you can access.

### Credit Score Tiers for DSCR Loans

| Credit Score | Approval Outlook | Typical Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **720+** | Strong | Best rates, 80% LTV at most programs |
| **700–719** | Good | Competitive rates, standard LTV |
| **680–699** | Acceptable | Minor rate adjustment |
| **640–679** | Conditional | Rate premium; some lenders require 25% down |
| **620–639** | Difficult | Limited lenders; often requires 1.25+ DSCR and 25–30% down |
| **Below 620** | Very limited | Most DSCR lenders decline; consider hard money bridge + refinance path |

### What Lenders Actually Review in Your Credit File

Beyond the score itself, underwriters check:

- **Mortgage payment history** — highest weight; a 30-day late on a mortgage in the last 12 months is often a deal-killer
- **Derogatory marks** — bankruptcies, foreclosures, deed-in-lieu, short sales
- **Delinquency pattern** — isolated late payments are treated differently than a pattern of lates
- **Open derogatory debt** — unpaid judgments, tax liens, collections

**Bankruptcies:** Most DSCR lenders require a seasoning period. Chapter 7 typically requires 2–4 years from discharge; Chapter 13 varies by lender and program [VERIFY: confirm seasoning requirements directly with your lender — guidelines vary and change]. Clean payment history *after* a bankruptcy can accelerate eligibility at some non-QM lenders.

## 3. Down Payment Requirements

DSCR loans are investment property loans — lenders won't accept FHA 3.5% or VA 0% down structures here. Expect to bring real equity.

### Standard Down Payment Ranges

- **20% down (80% LTV)** — baseline at most lenders for borrowers with 700+ credit and 1.0+ DSCR
- **25% down (75% LTV)** — typically required for: credit scores below 680, DSCR ratios below 1.0, first-time investors, multi-unit properties, or short-term rentals
- **30–35% down** — required at some lenders for high-risk combinations (lower credit + lower DSCR)

### Down Payment Sourcing Rules

Lenders apply strict rules to where your down payment comes from. Funds must be:

1. **Seasoned** — in your account for 30–60 days (lenders will request 2–3 months of bank statements)
2. **Yours** — gift funds are generally not accepted for investment properties
3. **Documented** — if funds originated from a property sale, provide the HUD-1 or closing disclosure

You *can* use proceeds from a cash-out refinance on another property as your down payment source — provided those funds are fully seasoned in your account before the new application. BRRRR investors use this frequently to recycle equity from one rental into the down payment for the next.

## 4. Appraisal Requirements

The DSCR loan appraisal does double duty: it confirms the property's market value *and* establishes its fair market rent. This rent determination drives your DSCR calculation — it's arguably the most consequential number in your entire file.

### Required Appraisal Forms

- **Form 1007** (Single-Unit Comparable Rent Schedule) — required for single-family rentals
- **Form 1025** (Small Residential Income Property Appraisal Report) — required for 2–4 unit properties
- **Full commercial appraisal** — typically required for 5+ unit multifamily

The appraiser surveys comparable rental properties nearby to determine what the subject property should reasonably rent for on the open market.

### Vacant vs. Occupied Properties

- **Currently leased:** Lenders use the **lower of** the actual lease amount or the appraiser's market rent estimate. Don't set rent above market rate before applying — you won't get credit for the overage.
- **Currently vacant:** Lenders use the appraiser's estimated market rent exclusively.

### Short-Term Rentals (Airbnb / VRBO)

Many DSCR lenders now accept short-term rental income, but methodology varies:
- Some use AirDNA data to estimate annualized income
- Some require a 12-month STR operating statement from the platform
- Some apply a discount to STR income relative to long-term rent to reflect vacancy risk

Not all DSCR programs allow STR properties — confirm with the lender before proceeding.

## 5. Cash Reserve Requirements

Lenders want to see that you can cover mortgage payments through a vacancy period or unexpected repair without defaulting. Reserves are a buffer — and a lender's insurance policy on your file.

### Standard Reserve Requirements

- **3 months PITIA** — minimum at most lenders
- **6 months PITIA** — commonly required for higher-risk profiles, no-ratio programs, or multi-unit properties
- **Per-property reserves** — if you're financing multiple DSCR loans simultaneously or already have a portfolio, some lenders require reserves on *every* financed property, not just the new one

### What Counts as Acceptable Reserves

| Asset Type | Counts as Reserves? |
|---|---|
| Checking / savings / money market | ✓ Full value |
| Stocks and investment accounts | ✓ Full or partial (after liquidity haircut) |
| 401(k) / IRA / retirement accounts | ✓ 60–70% of vested balance |
| Cash value of life insurance | ✓ |
| Real estate equity (not yet liquidated) | ✗ Does not count |
| Funds borrowed from another account | ✗ Does not count |

## 7. Closing in an LLC

One of DSCR loans' most investor-friendly features: the majority of DSCR lenders allow — and sometimes prefer — closing in the name of a business entity.

**Why investors use LLC closing:**
- **Liability protection** — separates personal assets from property-level risk
- **Portfolio organization** — each LLC can hold a separate property or market
- **Estate planning** — simplified ownership transfer
- **Conventional loan preservation** — keeping investment debt in an LLC may shield your personal DTI for future conventional financing

**What lenders require for LLC closing:**
- LLC in good standing in the subject property's state
- Articles of organization
- Operating agreement
- Proof of authority (who can sign for the entity)
- Personal guarantee from the managing member

Note: DSCR loans in an LLC are still **recourse** — you personally guarantee repayment even in the entity's name. This differs from true non-recourse commercial financing. That personal guarantee is the lender's backstop; the LLC provides liability protection for you, not risk reduction for the lender.

## Requirements by Investor Situation

DSCR qualification isn't one-size-fits-all. Here's how the requirements shape up for four common investor profiles:

### First-Time Investor
Expect more scrutiny. Some lenders require at least one prior real estate asset owned (investment or primary residence). Those that accept true first-timers often cap LTV at 75% and require a higher DSCR (1.25+) to offset experience risk. Prioritize a strong credit score (700+), a property in a liquid rental market with clear comparables, and clean down payment documentation.

### Experienced Portfolio Investor (5+ Properties)
DSCR loans are designed for you. Most programs have no maximum property count. Your lender will typically request a Schedule of Real Estate Owned (SREO) — a spreadsheet of all properties with address, value, mortgage balance, gross rent, and PITIA. Prepare this proactively; it dramatically speeds underwriting.

### Self-Employed Investor
This is DSCR's sweet spot. Your tax returns may show low net income after depreciation, business expenses, and write-offs — but none of that is relevant here. What matters is your credit profile, down payment reserves, and the subject property's income. Clean up your credit report and document down payment sources clearly.

### Short-Term Rental (STR) Operator
Verify STR eligibility explicitly before choosing a lender — not all DSCR programs accept Airbnb or VRBO income. Markets like the Smoky Mountains, Blue Ridge Mountains, coastal Southeast, and mountain resort areas tend to have fewer STR-eligible DSCR lenders, which concentrates demand among those that do. Ask lenders specifically: (1) Do you accept STR income? (2) What income documentation is required? (3) Do you apply a vacancy discount to STR income?

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the minimum DSCR ratio to qualify?**  
Most DSCR lenders require a minimum ratio of 1.0 — meaning rental income exactly covers the mortgage payment. Programs down to 0.75 exist but come with higher rates and larger down payment requirements. A DSCR of 1.25+ unlocks the best pricing.

**Do DSCR loans require tax returns or W-2s?**  
No. That's the defining feature of DSCR financing. Qualification is based on the rental property's income, not the borrower's personal income documentation. This is why DSCR loans are particularly popular with self-employed investors and full-time real estate professionals.

**Can I get a DSCR loan for an Airbnb property?**  
Many lenders now accept short-term rental income, typically using AirDNA data or a 12-month operating history from the rental platform. Not all DSCR programs allow STR — confirm explicitly with your lender before proceeding.

**Can I get a DSCR loan with a 600 credit score?**  
It's very difficult. Most programs start at 620, and at that floor you'll face limited program choices, higher rates, and potentially 25–30% down requirements. A credit score of 680+ meaningfully expands your options. If you're at 600, consider addressing credit issues first or exploring hard money bridge financing with a 6–12 month window to improve your profile.

**Are there prepayment penalties on DSCR loans?**  
Typically yes. Most DSCR loans include step-down prepayment penalties — commonly a 5-4-3-2-1 or 3-2-1 structure (e.g., 3% if you pay off in year 1, 2% in year 2, 1% in year 3). Accepting a prepayment penalty often allows you to buy down your interest rate. If you plan to sell or refinance before the penalty expires, run the numbers using the [Mortgage Calculator](/calculators/mortgage) to determine if the trade-off makes sense.

**What's the difference between DSCR and DTI?**  
DTI (Debt-to-Income ratio) compares your total personal monthly debt obligations to your gross personal income — it's used to underwrite conventional mortgages. DSCR compares the *property's* monthly income to the *property's* monthly debt payment. DSCR lenders largely set aside your personal DTI entirely, which is why these loans can feel dramatically simpler than conventional underwriting.

**Can I use a DSCR loan to refinance an existing rental property?**  
Yes. DSCR loans are available for both purchase and cash-out or rate-term refinances. For BRRRR investors, the DSCR refinance is the core exit strategy — you refinance out of a hard money loan once the property is rehabbed, tenanted, and generating stable rental income above the PITIA threshold.

**Can I close a DSCR loan in my LLC?**  
Yes, at most DSCR lenders. You'll sign a personal guarantee as the managing member, but the title and mortgage can be held in the entity name. This is one of DSCR's advantages over conventional investment loans, which often require personal title.
