# DSCR Loan Requirements: The Investor's Complete Qualification Guide (2026)

DSCR loans qualify you based on the property's rental income — not your W-2, tax returns, or debt-to-income ratio. To qualify, you typically need a minimum DSCR ratio of 1.0, a credit score of 620–680, and a 20–25% down payment. You don't need income documentation, there's no limit on how many properties you can finance, and you can close in an LLC. This guide covers every requirement in detail — including how lenders vary, how to calculate your ratio before you apply, and the fastest ways to fix a qualification shortfall.

---

## What Is a DSCR Loan?

DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio. A DSCR loan is an investment property mortgage that uses the property's rental income — not your personal income — to determine whether you qualify.

The core idea: if the rent covers the mortgage, you get the loan.

This makes DSCR the primary financing tool for:
- Self-employed investors whose tax returns understate income
- W-2 borrowers who've hit the 10-property conventional loan cap
- Investors scaling a portfolio quickly without re-qualifying on personal income each time
- Anyone buying a short-term rental (Airbnb/VRBO) or small multifamily property

DSCR loans are classified as Non-QM (non-qualified mortgage) because they fall outside the standard ability-to-repay documentation rules. That classification means slightly higher rates — typically 1–2% above conventional investment property rates — but far fewer restrictions on who can borrow and how many properties they can hold.

## The 5 Core DSCR Loan Requirements

### 1. Minimum DSCR Ratio

The DSCR ratio is calculated as:

**DSCR = Gross Monthly Rent ÷ Monthly PITIA**

*(PITIA = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + Association dues)*

| DSCR | What It Means | Typical Lender Response |
|---|---|---|
| 1.25+ | Property cash flows strongly | Best rates, highest LTV |
| 1.0–1.24 | Rent covers mortgage exactly or slightly exceeds it | Standard approval, normal pricing |
| 0.75–0.99 | Negative cash flow (property costs more than it earns) | "No-ratio" programs available; higher rate and larger down payment required |
| Below 0.75 | Significant shortfall | Most lenders will decline; hard money or bridge loan likely needed |

The industry-standard minimum is **DSCR of 1.0** — though lenders like Kiavi, Lima One Capital, and Griffin Funding all offer programs down to 0.75–0.80 at a rate premium. If you're evaluating a deal that's slightly underwater on cash flow but you believe in the appreciation or value-add story, ask specifically about no-ratio programs rather than assuming you're disqualified.

**Pro tip:** Run your DSCR *before* making an offer. Use [ProInvestorHub's Mortgage Calculator](/calculators/mortgage) to model PITIA at the likely loan amount, then check it against rental comps in your market. If you're close to 1.0, a slightly higher down payment can tip you over.

### 2. Credit Score Requirements

Unlike conventional mortgages where credit score is one of many factors, DSCR lenders weight it heavily because they can't verify your income. Think of credit score as the substitute signal.

**Minimum credit score by tier:**
- **620**: Minimum accepted by most DSCR lenders; expect higher rates, lower LTV (70–75%)
- **640**: Increasingly the de facto floor for competitive programs
- **680**: Standard threshold for full-program pricing and 80% LTV
- **720+**: Best rates, lowest down payment requirements, smoothest approval

A credit score in the 680–720 range and a DSCR of 1.25+ is the combination that gets you the most competitive terms across the widest set of lenders.

**If your score is below 680:** Focus on rapid improvement strategies — paying down revolving utilization below 30%, disputing errors, and avoiding new credit applications for 90 days before applying. Each 20-point improvement in score can reduce your rate by 0.25–0.375%.

### 3. Down Payment Requirements

DSCR loans require more down than owner-occupied mortgages. Because the lender isn't verifying your income, the equity you put in is part of their risk protection.

**Standard requirements:**
- **20% down (80% LTV)**: The most common program for borrowers with 680+ credit and DSCR ≥ 1.0
- **25% down (75% LTV)**: Often required for credit scores 640–679, or properties with DSCR 0.75–0.99
- **30–35% down**: Required for no-ratio programs or very low credit scores

**Sourcing matters.** Lenders require 30–60 days of bank statements showing the funds. Gift funds are typically not allowed for DSCR loans. The most common sources are:
- Savings or brokerage accounts (seasoned 60+ days)
- Cash-out refinance proceeds from an existing property
- Business account funds (LLC members)

### 4. Property Type and Appraisal Requirements

**Eligible property types** for most DSCR lenders:
- Single-family residences (SFR) — most common
- 2–4 unit multifamily (duplex, triplex, fourplex)
- Condominiums (warrantable and sometimes non-warrantable)
- Short-term rentals (Airbnb, VRBO) — lender-dependent

**Properties that typically don't qualify for standard DSCR:**
- 5+ unit apartment buildings (require commercial/portfolio financing)
- Properties in need of major structural rehab (use a fix-and-flip bridge loan, then refinance to DSCR)
- Raw land or non-income-producing properties

**The appraisal is dual-purpose.** Unlike conventional mortgages where the appraisal only confirms value, DSCR appraisals include a rent schedule (Form 1007 for SFR, Form 1025 for 2–4 units). The appraiser surveys comparable rentals in the area and provides a market rent opinion. If your property is vacant, the lender uses this estimate. If it's leased, most lenders use the *lower* of actual rent or appraised market rent.

This means **overpriced leases don't help you** — lenders cap DSCR calculations at market rent. And a vacant property isn't automatically disqualifying — you just need strong market rents to support the ratio.

### 5. Cash Reserves

After closing, most DSCR lenders require you to have liquid reserves on hand. This protects against vacancy, repairs, and missed payments.

**Typical reserve requirements:**
- **3 months PITIA**: Minimum for most standard programs
- **6 months PITIA**: Common for lower credit scores, no-ratio programs, or first-time investors
- **12 months PITIA**: Required by some lenders for short-term rental properties

Reserves must be liquid (savings, checking, money market) or near-liquid (securities accounts). Retirement accounts may count at a 70% haircut. Equity in other properties generally does not count as reserves.

## How to Calculate Your DSCR (Worked Example)

Let's say you're looking at a single-family rental in Atlanta:

- Purchase price: $280,000
- Down payment: 20% = $56,000
- Loan amount: $224,000
- Estimated rent: $2,100/month

**Step 1: Calculate monthly PITIA**
- Interest rate: 7.5% on 30-year fixed
- Principal + Interest: ~$1,567
- Property taxes: ~$230/month ($2,760/year)
- Insurance: ~$130/month
- HOA: $0
- **Total PITIA: $1,927/month**

**Step 2: Calculate DSCR**
- DSCR = $2,100 ÷ $1,927 = **1.09**

A DSCR of 1.09 qualifies under most standard programs. The property generates $173/month more than the mortgage costs — not rich cash flow, but enough to get approved.

## DSCR vs. Conventional Investment Loans: Which Should You Use?

**Choose conventional if:**
- You have strong W-2 income and room in your DTI
- You're buying your first 1–4 investment properties
- You want the lowest possible interest rate
- Your deal has thin cash flow that wouldn't support DSCR rates

**Choose DSCR if:**
- You're self-employed or have complex/variable income
- You've already used 4–10 conventional loans
- You want to close in an LLC
- You're buying a short-term rental
- You want to scale faster without re-qualifying each time

The rate premium for DSCR (typically 1–2% above conventional) is the key trade-off. On a $250,000 loan at 1.5% higher rate, that's roughly $300–400/month in additional interest — a real cost that should show up in your cash-flow analysis, not be glossed over. ## Short-Term Rental (STR) DSCR Considerations

Airbnb and VRBO properties can qualify for DSCR loans, but the underwriting works differently — and not every lender offers STR programs.

**Income calculation for STRs:**
- Lenders can't use projected nightly income at face value
- Most require an appraisal using a 12-month historical gross income figure (from AirDNA or platform statements)
- Some lenders use a blended approach: 75% of actual STR income or 100% of long-term market rent — whichever is lower

**What this means in practice:** A property that earns $60,000/year on Airbnb won't be counted at $5,000/month. Lenders typically apply a discount factor. If the property also makes strong economic sense as a long-term rental, that market rent estimate gives you a floor.

## 5 Common DSCR Disqualifiers and How to Fix Them

**1. DSCR below 1.0**
*Fix:* Increase down payment to lower the monthly PITIA. A 25% down payment vs. 20% on a $300K loan reduces PITIA by ~$200/month. Or negotiate a lower purchase price with rent comps in hand.

**2. Credit score below 640**
*Fix:* Pay down revolving balances below 30% of limits. Rapid rescoring (ask your loan officer) can update your score in 3–5 business days if the balances are already paid.

**3. Insufficient reserves**
*Fix:* A cash-out refinance on an existing property is the fastest way to generate liquid reserves. Even pulling $30,000 in equity from another rental can satisfy reserve requirements on the new purchase.

**4. Property type ineligible**
*Fix:* If the property needs major work, a bridge/fix-and-flip loan gets you in, then you refinance into DSCR once it's stabilized and rented. See the [BRRRR Calculator](/calculators/brrrr) to model this two-loan strategy.

**5. Rent doesn't support the DSCR ratio**
*Fix:* Either renegotiate the purchase price down, increase planned improvements that would justify higher rent, or look for a lender with a lower DSCR minimum (0.75–0.80 programs exist).

## DSCR Qualification Checklist

Before submitting an application, confirm you have:

- [ ] Credit score pulled (target 680+; minimum 620)
- [ ] Down payment funds sourced and seasoned 60+ days
- [ ] DSCR calculated at target loan amount (target 1.0+; model 1.25+ for best terms)
- [ ] Property type confirmed as DSCR-eligible (SFR, 2–4 unit, or STR lender confirmed)
- [ ] 3–6 months PITIA in liquid reserves after down payment and closing costs
- [ ] LLC formed if closing in business name (operating agreement and EIN required)
- [ ] Comparable rents researched to validate appraiser's market rent estimate
