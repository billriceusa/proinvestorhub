# DSCR Loan Requirements: The Investor's Complete 2026 Checklist

To qualify for a DSCR loan, your property's rental income must cover the mortgage payment (typically a 1.0 DSCR ratio minimum), your credit score must be at least 620–680 depending on the lender, and you'll need 20–25% down. Unlike conventional mortgages, there are no W-2s or personal income docs required — the property qualifies, not your paycheck. Here's exactly what lenders check and how to make sure you meet the bar before you apply.

---

## What Is a DSCR Loan (and Why It's Different)

A Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) loan is a mortgage specifically designed for investment properties. Instead of underwriting based on your personal income, tax returns, or debt-to-income ratio, the lender evaluates the property's ability to pay for itself.

The key formula:

> **DSCR = Gross Rental Income ÷ Total Monthly Mortgage Payment (PITIA)**

PITIA stands for Principal, Interest, Taxes, Insurance, and Association fees — every dollar the property costs each month.

A DSCR of 1.0 means the property exactly breaks even. A DSCR of 1.25 means rents are 25% higher than the total payment — most lenders consider this comfortable. A DSCR below 1.0 means the property doesn't technically cover its own debt, though some specialty programs will still lend on it at higher rates.

For context: a conventional investment mortgage requires full income documentation, W-2s, personal tax returns, and caps out at 10 financed properties. DSCR loans have no property count limit and require no personal income verification. That's why they've become the dominant financing vehicle for investors scaling a rental portfolio.

## The 6 Core DSCR Loan Requirements

### 1. DSCR Ratio: The Property Must Pay for Itself

Most lenders require a minimum DSCR of **1.0 to 1.25**, depending on the program tier:

| DSCR Range | What It Means | Lender Appetite |
|---|---|---|
| 1.25+ | Strong positive cash flow | Best rates, highest LTV available |
| 1.10–1.24 | Good cash flow | Standard program, competitive rates |
| 1.00–1.09 | Break-even | Approved, but higher rate or lower LTV may apply |
| 0.75–0.99 | Negative cash flow | Specialty "no-ratio" programs only — higher rates, larger down |
| Below 0.75 | Significant shortfall | Very few lenders; expect 30%+ down and premium rates |

**How the rent is calculated**: Lenders use the *lesser* of your actual signed lease or the appraiser's market rent estimate (from Form 1007). If the property is vacant, they use appraiser-estimated market rent. This matters in high-rent markets — your actual lease may be below market, but the appraiser sets the floor.

**Worked example**: You're buying a single-family rental for $350,000 with 20% down ($70,000). The DSCR loan carries a 7.5% rate on a 30-year fixed. Monthly PITIA (principal + interest + estimated taxes and insurance) = $2,180. Market rent per the appraisal = $2,400.

DSCR = $2,400 ÷ $2,180 = **1.10** ✓ Qualifies at standard terms.

### 2. Credit Score: The Floor Is 620, but 700+ Wins

DSCR loans don't verify income, so lenders lean harder on credit score as a risk signal. The spectrum by lender tier:

| Credit Score | Typical Outcome |
|---|---|
| 720+ | Best available rates; highest LTV (up to 80%); lowest reserves required |
| 700–719 | Competitive rates; standard LTV |
| 680–699 | Standard rates; may require slightly lower LTV or higher DSCR |
| 660–679 | Accessible; some lenders require 1.10+ DSCR to compensate |
| 640–659 | Limited lenders; expect rate premium and possible LTV reduction |
| 620–639 | Specialty lenders only (e.g., RCN Capital); typically 70–75% LTV max |
| Below 620 | Very few options; hard money or portfolio lender territory |

**Credit and DSCR interact**: A higher DSCR can sometimes offset a lower credit score, and vice versa. A borrower with a 640 credit score and a 1.30 DSCR may get better terms than a 640-score borrower at 1.05 DSCR.

---

### 3. Down Payment: Expect 20–25% (No Exceptions)

DSCR loans are investment property loans — there are no FHA, VA, or HomeReady low-down options here. The standard:

| Scenario | Typical Down Payment | Max LTV |
|---|---|---|
| Strong credit (700+) + DSCR 1.25+ | 20% | 80% |
| Standard program (660–699 credit) | 20–25% | 75–80% |
| Lower credit (620–659) | 25–30% | 70–75% |
| DSCR below 1.0 ("no-ratio" program) | 25–35% | 65–75% |
| Cash-out refinance | 25–30% equity retained | 70–75% LTV |

**Sourcing requirements**: Lenders will ask for 30–60 days of bank statements showing the down payment funds have been "seasoned" (sitting in your account, not just deposited). Gift funds are generally not allowed on DSCR loans. Equity from another property — accessed via a cash-out refinance or HELOC — is an acceptable source. ### 4. Cash Reserves: You Need a Safety Net After Closing

Most DSCR lenders require liquid reserves after closing — money in the bank to cover the mortgage if the property sits vacant or needs emergency repairs:

- **Standard requirement**: 3–6 months of PITIA in liquid reserves (cash, money market, or easily liquidated investments)
- **Higher-credit borrowers**: Some lenders reduce reserves to 2–3 months at 720+ credit
- **Multiple properties**: Lenders may require reserves for each financed property, not just the new one

**Reserves at closing example**: If your new property's PITIA is $2,200/month and the lender requires 6 months of reserves, you need $13,200 in liquid assets *after* you've paid the down payment and closing costs. Budget accordingly — this is a common surprise for first-time DSCR borrowers.

---

### 5. Property Eligibility: What Qualifies and What Doesn't

**Eligible**:
- Single-family rentals (1–4 units) — most common
- Condos and townhouses (warrantable and non-warrantable in some programs)
- Short-term rentals / Airbnb (many lenders now accept STR income; see below)
- Small multifamily (2–8 units on some programs)
- Properties titled in an LLC or business entity ✓

**Not eligible**:
- Owner-occupied properties (DSCR is investment-only)
- Properties needing major rehabilitation (use a bridge or fix-and-flip loan first, then refinance to DSCR)
- 5+ unit commercial multifamily (requires commercial/portfolio lending)
- Raw land or construction (use a construction loan; DSCR for stabilized properties only)

**Short-term rentals**: Many DSCR lenders now accept Airbnb/VRBO income, but underwriting varies significantly. Some lenders use AirDNA data to estimate STR income; others require a 12-month operating history; some use projected market rent as a conservative floor. If your deal depends on STR income to hit DSCR, specifically filter for STR-eligible lenders.

---

### 6. Documentation Required: Minimal, But Not Zero

Despite requiring no W-2s or tax returns, DSCR loans still require:

- **Credit report** (lender pulls tri-merge)
- **Property appraisal with Form 1007** (rent schedule — ordered by lender)
- **Signed lease agreement** (if property is tenant-occupied)
- **Proof of down payment funds** (30–60 days bank statements)
- **Entity documents** (if closing in LLC: Articles of Organization, Operating Agreement, EIN)
- **Insurance commitment** (hazard + flood if applicable; rent-loss insurance often required)
- **Prior investment experience** (some lenders want a schedule of real estate owned; not all require this)

What you **don't** need: W-2s, pay stubs, personal tax returns, employer verification, personal DTI calculation.

## How to Improve Your DSCR Before You Apply

If your deal is close but not quite qualifying — say, a 0.92 DSCR when the lender wants 1.0 — here are the levers you can pull:

### Increase Rental Income
- **Negotiate a higher rent** before the appraisal. The appraiser uses the lesser of actual lease or market rent — having a lease at or above market strengthens your DSCR calculation.
- **If vacant, price the property at market rate**. An appraisal on a vacant property uses estimated market rent. Make sure the property is priced correctly vs. comps.

### Reduce Monthly Payment
- **Put more down**: A larger down payment shrinks the loan balance and the monthly PI. Going from 20% to 25% down reduces the payment and can flip a marginal deal into a qualifying deal.
- **Buy down the rate**: Points paid upfront reduce your rate, lowering PI. Run the math — on a $300K loan, one point ($3,000) might save $50–70/month, improving DSCR by 0.02–0.03.
- **Choose interest-only**: Many DSCR lenders offer interest-only periods (5–10 years). Removing principal amortization significantly lowers the monthly obligation and boosts DSCR. [VERIFY: check IO availability with specific lender before relying on it in deal underwriting]

### Reduce Operating Cost Assumptions
- **Shop insurance**: Lenders use estimated insurance in PITIA. Getting actual insurance quotes before the appraisal — especially in high-rate states like Florida or Texas — can shift the DSCR calculation in your favor.
- **Verify tax estimates**: Property tax is estimated at purchase based on current assessed value. In many states (CA, TX, FL), the assessed value resets at sale. Lenders model the estimated post-sale tax. Make sure the lender is using accurate tax estimates, not inflated guesses.

### Use Cash-on-Cash and Cap Rate Together
Before you apply, make sure the deal actually pencils. Run the [Cash-on-Cash Calculator](/calculators/cash-on-cash) and [Cap Rate Calculator](/calculators/cap-rate) first. A deal with a 1.0 DSCR might still be a weak investment — DSCR qualification is the floor, not the goal.

## DSCR vs. Conventional Investment Loan: Which Is Right for You?

| Factor | DSCR Loan | Conventional Investment Mortgage |
|---|---|---|
| Income verification | None — property income qualifies | Full W-2/tax return docs required |
| DTI requirement | None | Typically 45% max |
| Property count limit | None | 10 financed properties max |
| Closing in LLC | ✓ Yes | ✗ Generally no |
| Rate (vs benchmark) | 1–2% premium | Closest to par |
| Down payment | 20–25% | 15–25% |
| Best for | Investors scaling past 4–10 properties, self-employed | First 1–2 properties with strong W-2 income |

The calculus is straightforward: if you're early in your career with strong W-2 income and fewer than 4 financed properties, conventional may give you a cheaper rate. Once you're self-employed, at 10+ properties, or need to close in an LLC, DSCR is almost always the right tool.

## Common Mistakes That Cause DSCR Denials

**1. Moving down payment funds right before application**
Lenders want 30–60 days of account statements showing the funds. A large, recent deposit raises flags. If you're liquidating stocks or transferring from another account, do it early.

**2. Underestimating property taxes**
In states where property tax resets at sale (most of the country), using pre-sale tax estimates overstates your DSCR. Always model with projected post-sale taxes.

**3. Buying property in personal name, then trying to transfer to LLC**
Most DSCR lenders allow LLC ownership at origination, but transferring after closing can trigger the due-on-sale clause. Decide before you close.

**4. Using STR income without a STR-eligible lender**
Not all DSCR programs accept Airbnb or VRBO income. If your DSCR depends on short-term rental income, confirm the lender explicitly supports STR underwriting before going under contract.

**5. Applying with only one lender**
DSCR loan rates and guidelines vary significantly. A 620-credit borrower who gets declined by Lender A (requiring 660) might easily qualify with Lender B. Rate shop with at least 2–3 lenders. ## Bottom Line

DSCR loan requirements come down to five things: a property that covers its own mortgage (1.0+ DSCR), a credit score of 620–680+, 20–25% down, 3–6 months of cash reserves, and an eligible investment property. The paperwork is minimal compared to conventional financing — no W-2s, no tax returns, no DTI calculation.

The single best thing you can do before applying is run your numbers. Know your DSCR. Know your credit score. Know exactly how much you need in the bank at closing.
