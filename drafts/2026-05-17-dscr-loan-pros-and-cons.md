# DSCR Loan Pros and Cons: A Lending Veteran's Decision Framework

DSCR loans let real estate investors qualify based on a property's rental income — not their personal W-2s or tax returns. For investors who are self-employed, holding properties in an LLC, or pushing past the 10-loan conventional limit, that's genuinely game-changing. But DSCR loans aren't for everyone. They carry higher rates, larger down payments, and qualification rules that can torpedo a deal if the numbers don't work. This guide cuts through the lender pitches to give you a clear-eyed view of when a DSCR loan wins — and when it doesn't.

## What Is a DSCR Loan?

A Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) loan is a non-QM mortgage that evaluates a rental property's ability to cover its own debt payments. Lenders don't ask for your tax returns, W-2s, or pay stubs. Instead, they look at one number: does the property's income cover its monthly debt obligations?

The formula is straightforward:

**DSCR = Monthly Gross Rent ÷ Monthly PITIA**

*(PITIA = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + HOA)*

A DSCR of 1.0 means the rent exactly covers the payment. A DSCR of 1.25 means the rent covers 125% of the payment — the lender's preferred buffer. Most lenders want to see 1.0 or above, though some will go down to 0.75–0.80 for strong borrowers with substantial reserves.

## The Pros of DSCR Loans

### 1. No Personal Income Verification

This is the headline feature, and it's real. DSCR lenders don't require W-2s, tax returns, or employment verification. If you're self-employed, own a business with complex write-offs, or retired with investment income, conventional lenders often misread your income picture entirely. DSCR sidesteps that problem by letting the property speak for itself.

**Who this matters for most:** Self-employed investors, high-write-off business owners, retirees, and anyone whose taxable income looks artificially low on paper.

### 2. Close in an LLC or Trust

Most conventional mortgages require you to hold title personally — you'd have to transfer the property to an LLC after closing, which can trigger a due-on-sale clause. DSCR lenders routinely allow title to be held in an LLC, trust, or other entity from day one. That's cleaner liability protection and better estate planning without the paperwork gymnastics.

### 3. No Cap on the Number of Properties

Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac conventional loans cap you at 10 financed properties. Once you hit that limit, conventional financing is off the table. DSCR lenders have no such restriction. As long as each property qualifies on its own cash flow, you can keep stacking. This makes DSCR loans the workhorse product for investors scaling from 5 to 50 doors.

### 4. Faster Closings

Without income verification paperwork, DSCR underwriting is more streamlined. Experienced DSCR lenders can close in 21–30 days, sometimes faster. That matters in competitive markets where a 45-day conventional close loses deals.

### 5. Flexible Loan Structures

DSCR lenders offer several structures that conventional loans don't:
- **30-year fixed-rate** — the most common, predictable payment
- **Interest-only periods** — lower monthly payment, improves DSCR on borderline deals, useful for value-add properties
- **5/6 or 7/6 ARMs** — lower initial rate for investors planning a shorter hold
- **40-year amortization** — some lenders offer this to stretch payments and boost DSCR

### 6. BRRRR's Best Friend

If you're using the [BRRRR strategy](/blog/hard-money-vs-dscr-loans) — Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat — DSCR is the natural refinance vehicle. After you stabilize the property with a tenant and the rent is flowing, a DSCR loan lets you pull out your equity based on the property's performance, not your personal balance sheet. Many BRRRR investors complete the cycle from hard money acquisition to DSCR permanent financing in 6–12 months.

### 7. Cash-Out Refinance Available

DSCR cash-out refinances let you tap equity in stabilized rentals — typically up to 75% LTV — without income documentation. For investors who are equity-rich but cash-light, this unlocks capital for the next deal without selling an asset.

## The Cons of DSCR Loans

### 1. Higher Interest Rates

Expect to pay 1.0%–2.5% more than a comparable conventional loan. In May 2026, that typically means DSCR rates in the 7.0%–9.0% range for 30-year fixed, depending on your credit score, LTV, and market. [VERIFY: Confirm current DSCR rate range with your lender before publishing.]

That premium has real consequences for cash flow. On a $300,000 loan, a 1.5% rate difference adds roughly $270/month to your payment — which can flip a 1.15 DSCR deal to a 0.95 DSCR deal overnight if you're not accounting for it at purchase.

### 2. Larger Down Payment Required

DSCR lenders typically require 20%–25% down, compared to 15%–20% for conventional investment property loans. Some lenders go to 25%–30% on multi-family or properties with weaker DSCR ratios. The capital requirement is real and limits how fast you can scale without recycling equity.

### 3. Prepayment Penalties

Most DSCR loans carry step-down prepayment penalties — typically 3–5 years. A common structure is 5-4-3-2-1: 5% of the loan balance if you sell or refinance in year one, declining by 1% each year. If you're planning a short hold or a quick flip to a long-term strategy, this needs to be modeled into your exit.

### 4. The Property Must Cash Flow — Period

On a conventional loan, your personal income backstops the lender's risk. On a DSCR loan, the property has to stand alone. Vacant properties, properties with below-market rents, or deals in high-cost markets where rent doesn't cover a 25% down DSCR payment simply won't qualify. If your deal barely pencils at current rates, DSCR isn't going to rescue it.

### 5. Stricter Reserve Requirements

Many DSCR lenders require 3–12 months of PITIA in liquid reserves after closing — sometimes more for multi-family or investors with multiple financed properties. This can be a meaningful capital tie-up if you're running lean.

## How to Calculate DSCR on Your Deal: Two Worked Examples

### Example A: A Clean Deal (DSCR 1.28)

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Purchase Price | $280,000 |
| Down Payment (25%) | $70,000 |
| Loan Amount | $210,000 |
| Rate (30-yr fixed) | 7.75% |
| Monthly P&I | $1,505 |
| Monthly Taxes + Insurance + HOA | $450 |
| **Total Monthly PITIA** | **$1,955** |
| **Market Monthly Rent** | **$2,500** |
| **DSCR** | **1.28** |

At 1.28, this deal clears most lenders' minimums comfortably. You'd likely qualify with a 620+ credit score and standard reserves.

### Example B: A Borderline Deal (DSCR 0.92)

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Purchase Price | $350,000 |
| Down Payment (25%) | $87,500 |
| Loan Amount | $262,500 |
| Rate (30-yr fixed) | 8.25% |
| Monthly P&I | $1,972 |
| Monthly Taxes + Insurance + HOA | $580 |
| **Total Monthly PITIA** | **$2,552** |
| **Market Monthly Rent** | **$2,350** |
| **DSCR** | **0.92** |

At 0.92, this deal falls below a 1.0 DSCR threshold. Most standard DSCR programs will decline it. Options: (a) negotiate the purchase price down, (b) increase the down payment to reduce the loan balance, (c) find a lender who accepts sub-1.0 DSCR with compensating factors (higher credit score, substantial reserves), or (d) walk away.

## When a DSCR Loan Is the Right Choice

Use a DSCR loan when:

- **You're self-employed or have a complex income profile** and a conventional lender would penalize your write-offs
- **You're buying in an LLC** and don't want to deal with post-close title transfers
- **You already have 10+ conventional loans** and need non-Fannie/Freddie financing
- **You're completing a BRRRR cycle** and need to refinance out of hard money into permanent financing
- **The property's rent-to-payment ratio is strong** (DSCR 1.2+) and you're planning a 5+ year hold
- **You want to scale a portfolio** without each new property triggering a full personal income underwrite

---

## When a DSCR Loan Is the Wrong Choice

Don't reach for a DSCR loan when:

- **The deal barely cash flows.** If DSCR is hovering around 1.0–1.05, one vacancy or rate increase breaks the deal. A conventional loan with lower rates may give you more margin.
- **You're flipping or holding short-term.** DSCR's prepayment penalties make short holds expensive. [Hard money](/blog/hard-money-vs-dscr-loans) is built for that.
- **Your credit score is below 620.** DSCR lenders generally require a minimum credit score of 620–660. Below that, hard money or private lenders are your path.
- **You're buying a primary residence.** DSCR is investor-only. Full stop.
- **The loan is under $100K–$150K.** Most DSCR lenders have minimum loan sizes in this range. Smaller deals may need local community banks or portfolio lenders.

## What Lenders Actually Look At (Beyond the DSCR Ratio)

After 30+ years in lending, here's what underwriters focus on that most articles ignore:

**Credit score tiers matter more than you think.** A 740+ score unlocks materially better rates — sometimes 0.5%–0.75% lower — which can push a borderline DSCR deal to qualifying. A 620 score is the floor at most shops, and you'll pay for it.

**Lease vs. appraiser-estimated rent.** Some lenders use the actual lease amount; others use a 1007 rent schedule from the appraisal. If your market rent is above current lease rates, the appraisal-based approach can increase your qualifying DSCR.

**Property type adjustments.** Multi-family (2–4 units) often gets a stricter LTV and rate than single-family. Short-term rental income (Airbnb/VRBO) requires documented 12-month history or AirDNA projections — not all lenders accept STR income for DSCR purposes.

**Reserves.** Strong reserves (12+ months PITIA) can compensate for a slightly lower DSCR. If you're at 0.95 DSCR but have $80K in post-close reserves, some lenders will approve it.

## How DSCR Fits With Your Cap Rate Analysis

DSCR and cap rate measure different things and both belong in every deal analysis.

- **[Cap rate](/calculators/cap-rate)** tells you how the property performs independent of financing — it's a property efficiency metric. A 6.5% cap rate is meaningful regardless of how you finance the deal.
- **DSCR** tells you whether you can finance the deal at all, and with what structure. A 6.5% cap rate property might have a DSCR of 1.3 at 25% down and 7.5% — or 0.85 at 20% down and 8.5%. Financing terms change everything.

Not all DSCR lenders are created equal. Rate ranges vary by 1%–2% across lenders for identical borrower profiles. Minimum DSCR thresholds, reserve requirements, and property type acceptance differ significantly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the minimum DSCR to qualify for a loan?

Most lenders require a minimum DSCR of 1.0, meaning the property's rent exactly covers the PITIA payment. Some lenders accept sub-1.0 DSCR (down to 0.75–0.80) with compensating factors like a high credit score, large down payment, or substantial reserves. A 1.25+ DSCR is considered strong and typically unlocks better rates.

### Can I get a DSCR loan with a 600 credit score?

Most DSCR lenders set a floor of 620–640. A 600 score will likely disqualify you at standard DSCR programs. Your alternatives at that credit level would be hard money lenders (which focus on asset value rather than borrower credit) or private/portfolio lenders.

### Are DSCR loans available for multi-family properties?

Yes. DSCR loans typically cover 1–4 unit residential properties, and some lenders extend to 5–10 units under commercial DSCR programs. Rates and LTV requirements are generally tighter on multi-family (2–4 units), with lenders often requiring 25%–30% down and a 1.2+ DSCR.

### How do DSCR loans handle short-term rental income?

This varies by lender. Some DSCR lenders accept STR income based on a 12-month trailing average from platforms like Airbnb or VRBO. Others require AirDNA market-rate projections or a 1007 long-term rent schedule from the appraiser (which may be lower than actual STR income). If your deal depends on Airbnb rates, confirm the lender's STR policy before ordering an appraisal.

### What is the typical rate premium for a DSCR loan over a conventional loan?

Expect 1.0%–2.5% above conventional investment property loan rates. The gap narrows with higher credit scores, lower LTV, and stronger DSCR ratios. [VERIFY: Confirm current rate spread with 2-3 lenders before publishing.]
