# HELOC on Investment Property: The Investor's Decision Framework (2026 Guide)

You can get a HELOC on an investment property — but most banks quietly won't do it. Lenders that do require at least 25–30% equity, a credit score of 700+, and will charge a rate 1–3 points above their primary-residence HELOC. Before you waste time applying at the wrong places, here's exactly how investment property HELOCs work, who offers them, and when they actually make sense versus other equity-leverage strategies.

---

## Primary Home HELOC vs. Investment Property HELOC: Start Here

Most investors never need to answer this question: *which property do I pull from?* The answer is almost always your primary home — and that's fine. The two products are structurally different in ways that matter.

| Factor | Primary Residence HELOC | Investment Property HELOC |
|---|---|---|
| Lender availability | Most banks, credit unions | Specialty lenders, portfolio lenders, some credit unions |
| Max LTV | 85–90% CLTV | 70–75% CLTV |
| Min credit score | 620–660 | 700–720 |
| Rate premium | Prime + 0–1% | Prime + 1–3% |
| Income documentation | Standard W-2 or tax returns | Rental income + personal income both reviewed |
| Approx. rates (2026) | 7–9% variable | 9–13% variable |

**The investor math implication:** If your primary home has $200,000 in equity and your rental property also has $200,000 in equity, you can almost certainly pull more — cheaper — from the primary home. The only reason to go the investment property route is if you've already tapped your primary home, or if you want to protect your personal residence from any business-side risk.

## What Most Banks Won't Tell You About Investment Property HELOCs

The dirty secret: the vast majority of banks on your street — Chase, Wells Fargo, Bank of America — either don't offer HELOCs on investment properties at all, or quietly stopped offering them post-2022 as rate volatility made these second-lien products unattractive. Chase's own website, for instance, notes that HELOCs are not available in all states and their articles are explicitly "for educational purposes only" — meaning they're not necessarily selling the product.

Where do you actually find investment property HELOCs in 2026?

- **Portfolio lenders and community banks** — Banks that hold their own loans (rather than sell to Fannie/Freddie) have the most flexibility. They can create custom second-lien products on rentals. Call your local community bank directly.
- **Credit unions** — Credit unions are the most common source of investment property HELOCs today. Many have a niche in this space because they serve local real estate investors.
- **Specialty non-QM lenders** — Some non-QM lenders offer investment property HELOCs, though you'll pay for the flexibility.

## Investment Property HELOC Requirements: The Real Numbers

Based on what lenders are actually underwriting in 2026, here's what you need to qualify:

### Equity (LTV)
Most lenders cap at 70–75% CLTV (combined loan-to-value). This means:

**Example:** Rental property worth $350,000. Existing mortgage balance: $180,000.
- Max CLTV at 75%: $350,000 × 0.75 = $262,500
- Less existing mortgage: $262,500 − $180,000 = **$82,500 max HELOC**

If your existing mortgage balance is higher relative to value, you may not have enough equity to qualify.

### Credit Score
Expect a minimum of 700, with better pricing at 740+. Unlike DSCR loans — which primarily qualify based on the property's rental income — HELOC lenders almost always pull personal credit and review personal DTI.

### Debt-to-Income (DTI)
Most lenders want your total DTI (including all mortgages, the new HELOC payment, and personal debts) below 43–50%. This is where investors with large portfolios often hit a wall — too many mortgages, even profitable ones, can tip DTI over the threshold. [VERIFY: confirm 2026 DTI thresholds with current lender underwriting guidelines]

### Rental Income Documentation
You'll need signed leases, 12–24 months of bank statements showing rental deposits, and likely current rent rolls if you own multiple units. Lenders typically credit 75% of gross rental income toward qualifying income (a 25% vacancy/expense buffer).

### Cash Reserves
Expect lenders to require 6–12 months of PITI (principal, interest, taxes, insurance) reserves for the investment property.

## How Active Investors Actually Use a HELOC on Investment Property

Banks write about HELOCs for "home improvement" and "debt consolidation." That's not why real estate investors use them. Here's how the strategy actually plays out:

### 1. The BRRRR Refinance Supplement

In the [BRRRR method](/blog/brrrr-method-complete-guide) (Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat), the refinance step typically gets you 75% of ARV. If your rehab added enough value, you pull out most of your initial cash. But what about that remaining 25% equity you can't refinance out?

A HELOC on the now-stabilized property gives you revolving access to that trapped equity — without triggering a new appraisal or paying full DSCR loan closing costs again. Use our [BRRRR Calculator](/calculators/brrrr) to model how much equity you should expect to leave in a deal after the refinance.

### 2. The Down Payment Bridge

Scenario: You've found a cash-flowing rental in a strong market. You need $60,000 for a 20% down payment on a $300,000 property. Instead of sitting on the sidelines while you save, you draw $60,000 from your investment property HELOC, make the purchase, then use the new property's cash flow to service both the DSCR mortgage and the HELOC draw.

Run the numbers on your new deal with the [cash-on-cash calculator](/calculators/cash-on-cash) to confirm the combined debt service still leaves you cash-flow positive.

### 3. Force-Equity Extraction Between Deals

You've completed a value-add on a rental that appraises $80,000 higher than you paid. You don't want to do a full cash-out refinance (rates are higher than your existing mortgage, plus closing costs). A HELOC lets you access that forced equity on a revolving, interest-only basis during the draw period — a much cheaper bridge than a hard money loan.

**Cost comparison on a $60,000 draw:**
- Hard money loan: 11–14% + 2–3 points = ~$7,000+ annualized
- Investment property HELOC: 10–11% variable, interest-only = ~$6,000–$6,600/year, no points

The HELOC wins if you need the money for more than a few months. For 30–90 day bridges, [hard money](/blog/hard-money-vs-dscr-loans) may still be faster to close.

## The HELOC Arbitrage Test: Does the Math Work?

The core question: does the spread between your HELOC rate and your investment return justify the risk?

**Scenario:** You draw $80,000 from an investment property HELOC at 10.5% variable.
- Annual interest cost: ~$8,400
- You invest in a rental property generating 7% cash-on-cash: $80,000 × 7% = $5,600/year

**Net: -$2,800/year.** Cash-on-cash return doesn't cover the HELOC cost. You need appreciation or equity capture (BRRRR-style) to justify it.

Now model it differently:
- Same $80,000 HELOC draw
- You use it as down payment on a 4-unit multifamily that generates $1,100/month net NOI
- Annual NOI: $13,200
- Less HELOC interest: $8,400
- **Net: +$4,800/year** — plus principal paydown on the property's DSCR loan + any appreciation

Check the [cap rate calculator](/calculators/cap-rate) to evaluate NOI on target properties before drawing from your HELOC. The deal needs to generate enough NOI to cover the HELOC carrying cost with meaningful margin.

## Investment Property HELOC vs. Cash-Out Refinance: Which Is Right?

This is the most common decision investors face when looking to access equity.

| | Investment Property HELOC | Cash-Out Refinance |
|---|---|---|
| Closing costs | $500–$2,000 (or waived) | 2–4% of loan amount |
| Rate structure | Variable (risky in rising rate env.) | Fixed available |
| Rate level | Higher (9–12% in 2026) | Higher if above-market rate (7–9% DSCR) |
| Best for | Short-term draws, revolving needs | Large permanent capital need |
| Preserves existing mortgage? | Yes ✓ | No ✗ |
| Tax deductibility of interest | Only if used for property improvements [VERIFY] | Interest deductible as rental expense |

**Rule of thumb:** If your existing mortgage rate is below 6.5%, keep it and use a HELOC for short-term capital. If you need $100,000+ for a permanent capital deployment, run the math on a cash-out refinance — see our [guide to refinancing investment property](/blog/how-to-refinance-investment-property).

For rental portfolio investors, a [DSCR cash-out refinance](/lenders/dscr-loans) often comes out ahead for large draws: you get a fixed rate, longer amortization, and the property's rental income qualifies you — not your personal W-2.

## Tax Implications of Investment Property HELOCs

Interest on a HELOC used for a rental property is generally deductible as an ordinary business expense under IRC Section 162 — not subject to the $750,000 mortgage interest limitation that applies to personal residences. This means the full interest cost of your HELOC, if used for rental property expenses, improvements, or acquiring additional investment properties, should be deductible against rental income.

[VERIFY: Confirm with current IRS guidance and consult a real estate CPA — tax law changes can affect this treatment, and documentation of HELOC use for rental purposes is essential.]

Keep your HELOC proceeds in a dedicated account and document every draw to its rental business purpose. Co-mingling personal and investment draws on the same HELOC complicates deductibility.

## Alternatives When You Can't Qualify for an Investment Property HELOC

If lenders decline your investment property HELOC application (or the rate is simply too high), here are the most practical alternatives:

**1. Primary Residence HELOC** — Cheaper rate, easier to get. Higher personal risk since your home is collateral. Viable if the investment makes sense on the math and you have strong personal liquidity.

**2. DSCR Cash-Out Refinance** — Available through our [DSCR lender directory](/lenders/dscr-loans). No personal income required; qualifies on the property's lease income. Best for $100,000+ permanent draws where you can accept a new first-lien rate.

**3. Portfolio Cross-Collateral Line** — Some portfolio lenders will create a blanket line of credit across 3–10 of your properties. More complex, but unlocks equity at portfolio scale. Best accessed through a mortgage broker who specializes in investor portfolios.

**4. Seller Financing or Subject-To** — For acquisition funding specifically, seller-financing structures can eliminate the HELOC need entirely. Outside scope here but worth noting for investors facing equity constraints.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can you use a HELOC from an investment property to buy another investment property?**

Yes. There's no restriction on how you use HELOC proceeds. Many investors use investment property HELOCs as revolving down payment capital — draw to buy, rent out the new property, use cash flow to pay back the HELOC, draw again.

**Does a HELOC on an investment property affect my DTI for future loans?**

Yes. The monthly interest payment on your HELOC draws will show up on your credit report and count against your DTI. A HELOC with a $80,000 balance at 10.5% adds ~$700/month to your DTI calculation — factor that into how future conventional or DSCR loan applications will look.

**How long does it take to get a HELOC on an investment property?**

Longer than a primary residence HELOC. Expect 30–60 days with most lenders. An appraisal is almost always required (unlike some primary-residence HELOCs that use automated valuation models). Plan your deal timeline accordingly.

**What happens to my HELOC when I sell the investment property?**

The HELOC is a lien on the property. It must be paid off at closing. You cannot sell the property without satisfying the outstanding HELOC balance. Plan your exit accordingly — do the HELOC payoff calculation before you list.

**Can I get a HELOC on a property I just bought?**

Most lenders have seasoning requirements of 6–12 months before they'll underwrite a HELOC on an investment property. You generally can't purchase and HELOC-extract in the same quarter — a key reason some investors prefer the [BRRRR refinance](/blog/brrrr-method-complete-guide) model, which uses a DSCR cash-out refi instead.
