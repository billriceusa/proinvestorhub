# DSCR Loan Requirements in 2026: The Complete Investor's Checklist

If you're evaluating a DSCR loan for an investment property, the short answer on requirements is: your credit score should be at least 620–680, you'll need 20–25% down, and the property must generate enough rental income to cover the mortgage payment (a DSCR ratio of 1.0 or higher). No W-2s, no tax returns, no personal income verification needed. That's the baseline. But lenders vary significantly on where they draw each line, and knowing the full picture before you apply saves you from declined applications and rate surprises.

This guide covers every requirement — the ones lenders publish and the ones they don't — so you can walk into an application ready to close.

---

## What Makes DSCR Loans Different

A DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) loan qualifies you based on what the **property earns**, not what you earn. Instead of reviewing your W-2s, tax returns, and personal debt-to-income ratio, the lender asks one question: does the property's rental income cover the mortgage payment?

This makes DSCR loans the dominant product for:
- Self-employed investors whose tax returns understate income
- Investors who've already hit the conventional loan limit (10 properties per Fannie/Freddie)
- Investors closing in an LLC or entity name
- Anyone scaling a rental portfolio faster than their personal income allows

The trade-off: DSCR rates typically run 1–2% higher than conventional investment property loans. You're paying for flexibility. Whether that's worth it depends on your deal's numbers — run them through the [rental cashflow calculator](/calculators/rental-cashflow) before you commit to a loan structure.

## Core DSCR Loan Requirements

### 1. DSCR Ratio: The One Requirement That Determines Everything

The DSCR ratio is calculated as:

**DSCR = Gross Monthly Rent ÷ Monthly PITIA**

PITIA = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + (HOA if applicable)

**What lenders require:**
- **Standard programs:** 1.0 DSCR minimum — the property breaks even
- **Premium programs:** 1.25+ DSCR earns the best rates and highest LTV
- **Stretch programs:** Some lenders offer 0.75–0.80 DSCR programs at higher rates and lower LTV

A property renting for $2,500/month with a $2,000 PITIA has a DSCR of 1.25 — a comfortable approval. A property at $2,000 rent and $2,200 PITIA has a DSCR of 0.91 — that's a no-go at standard programs, though some lenders have "no-ratio" products for the right borrower profile.

**For short-term rentals (Airbnb/VRBO):** Many lenders now accept projected market rent from an appraisal or STR revenue estimates from platforms like AirDNA when no lease is in place. This is property-specific — not all lenders offer it, so if your deal is an Airbnb, filter for STR-capable lenders when you [compare DSCR lenders](/lenders/dscr-loans).

### 2. Credit Score Requirements

DSCR lenders care about credit score because it directly determines your rate tier — not just whether you qualify.

| Credit Score | Typical Access |
|---|---|
| 740+ | Best rates, highest LTV (up to 80%), most lender options |
| 700–739 | Good rates, standard LTV |
| 680–699 | Qualifies at most lenders, slight rate premium |
| 640–679 | Qualifies at many non-QM lenders; expect 0.25–0.50% rate increase |
| 620–639 | Limited lender pool; some lenders require compensating factors |
| Below 620 | Very few DSCR options; typically need hard money or portfolio loans |

The minimum score most often cited in the market is **640**. Some lenders go to 620 with strong compensating factors (high DSCR, large reserves). A handful of programs go lower in exchange for significant rate premiums.

Bottom line: if your score is below 680, shop carefully. A 40-point difference in credit score can mean a full percentage point difference in rate on a DSCR loan, which on a $300K loan changes your monthly payment by roughly $170 and your annual cash flow by $2,000.

### 3. Down Payment Requirements

Standard DSCR down payment is **20–25%** of the purchase price.

What influences where you land:
- **DSCR ratio:** Higher DSCR → some lenders allow lower down payment or higher LTV
- **Credit score:** 740+ may unlock 80% LTV; sub-680 often requires 25–30% down
- **Property type:** 2–4 unit properties and short-term rentals typically require 25% down vs. 20% for SFRs
- **Loan amount:** Jumbo DSCR loans (above $1–2M) generally require 25–30% down

One strategy worth knowing: **DSCR cash-out refinance on an existing property** to fund the down payment on a new acquisition. If you own a property with equity, you can pull that equity as your down payment, keeping your liquid capital free. This is the BRRRR method's long-term hold exit — and it's a major reason DSCR loans pair so well with that strategy.

### 4. Property Type Eligibility

Most DSCR programs accept:
- **Single-family rentals (SFR):** Most common, widest lender availability
- **2–4 unit multifamily:** Available with most lenders; requires 25% down at most programs
- **Condos:** Available; some lenders restrict non-warrantable condos
- **Short-term rentals:** Available with a growing subset of lenders; requires STR-capable underwriting

What typically doesn't qualify for DSCR:
- 5+ unit commercial properties (need commercial or portfolio loans instead)
- Owner-occupied primary residences (DSCR is investment property only)
- Land or development projects
- Fix-and-flip projects (these use hard money or bridge loans)

If your property is a 5+ unit building, see the [lender finder](/lenders/finder) — it can filter for portfolio and commercial lenders who handle those property types.

### 5. Loan Amounts

Most DSCR programs operate between **$75,000 and $3–5 million**. Some lenders have higher minimums ($100K or $150K). This rules out DSCR for very low-cost market properties — in those cases, local portfolio lenders or community banks are often better fits.

## Beyond the Basics: What Else Lenders Evaluate

The four requirements above determine baseline eligibility. These factors determine your **rate and terms**:

### Cash Reserves

Most DSCR lenders require **3–6 months of PITIA in reserves** (liquid assets) after closing. Some programs require more for 2–4 unit properties or larger loan amounts. Reserves prove you can service the debt through a vacancy period.

Acceptable reserve sources: checking/savings, investment accounts (stocks, ETFs at typically 70–80% of value), retirement accounts (50–70%), crypto (at some lenders, with significant discounts).

### Appraisal and Market Rent Determination

The appraisal does two jobs on a DSCR loan:
1. Confirms the property value (for LTV calculation)
2. Establishes market rent via a rent schedule (for DSCR calculation if there's no lease)

If you have a signed lease, lenders typically use the lesser of the lease amount or appraised market rent. If the property is vacant, they use the appraised market rent only. This means your DSCR is appraiser-dependent — if the market rent estimate comes in lower than you modeled, your DSCR drops. Plan for this in your deal analysis using the [rental cashflow calculator](/calculators/rental-cashflow).

### Property Condition

DSCR lenders require the property to be habitable at closing — no significant deferred maintenance, no structural issues, and compliant with local codes. Properties needing significant repairs typically need to go through a hard money or renovation loan first (like a BRRRR acquisition), then refinance into DSCR once the property is stabilized.

## How Requirements Vary Across Lender Types

This is the piece most articles skip. Not all DSCR lenders are the same — they have meaningfully different requirements depending on their capital source and risk appetite.

**Non-QM DSCR Lenders** (the specialty investment property lenders — like those in the [ProInvestorHub DSCR lender directory](/lenders/dscr-loans)): These are the most flexible. They underwrite DSCR loans at scale, accept LLCs, allow STR income, and often have the widest credit score range. They sell loans into private securitization markets rather than Fannie/Freddie. This flexibility is their core product.

**Portfolio Lenders / Community Banks:** Hold loans on their own balance sheet. Often more flexible on unusual property types or deals that don't fit cookie-cutter criteria. Can be harder to find and have less standardized guidelines — you often have to call rather than apply online.

**Debt Funds / Hard Money Lenders Offering DSCR Products:** Some bridge/hard money lenders have added 30-year DSCR products. Often higher rates but faster closes and more situational flexibility.

## DSCR Loan Requirements Checklist

Use this before you apply:

**Property:**
- [ ] Single-family, 2–4 unit, condo, or STR-eligible property
- [ ] Property is habitable (no major deferred maintenance)
- [ ] Estimated rent / active lease on file
- [ ] Projected DSCR ≥ 1.0 (calculate using gross rent ÷ full PITIA)

**Borrower:**
- [ ] Credit score 620+ (640+ preferred; 680+ for best terms)
- [ ] Down payment: 20–25% of purchase price available
- [ ] Closing reserves: 3–6 months of PITIA available post-close
- [ ] No recent bankruptcies (typically 2–4 year seasoning required)
- [ ] No recent foreclosures (typically 3–5 year seasoning required)

**Loan Structure:**
- [ ] Loan amount ≥ lender minimum (typically $75K–$150K)
- [ ] Loan amount ≤ lender maximum ($2M–$5M for most programs)
- [ ] LLC or personal name — confirm lender accepts your preferred structure
- [ ] Prepayment penalty terms reviewed and acceptable for your exit timeline

## Common Reasons DSCR Loans Get Declined

Knowing these upfront prevents wasted time:

**1. DSCR below 1.0 at appraisal.** The deal penciled at 1.1 DSCR, but the appraisal came in with lower market rents. Solution: know your appraisal risk before rate-locking, or use the property's actual executed lease if one is in place.

**2. Credit score below the program minimum.** Lenders pull all three bureaus and use the middle score. A single derogatory item can put you below a 640 threshold. Pull your credit before applying and dispute any errors.

**3. Insufficient reserves.** Closing without enough cash in the bank for the required reserve period is a common stumbling block for investors who used most of their capital on the down payment. Plan reserves separately from your down payment.

**4. Property condition issues.** An appraisal flags a leaky roof or non-functional HVAC. The lender requires repairs before close. Solution: physical inspection before applying, and budget for any needed work.

**5. Wrong lender for your property type.** Applied to a lender who doesn't do STR income or 3–4 unit properties. Solution: filter for lenders by property type before applying — the [lender finder](/lenders/finder) does this automatically.

## How to Qualify If You're On the Edge

If one or two requirements are a stretch, these strategies help:

**Low credit score (640–680):** Focus on lenders who explicitly advertise 640-minimum programs. Be prepared for a 0.25–0.50% rate premium. More impactfully, consider spending 3–6 months before applying to clean up any reporting errors and let recent positive history build.

**Borderline DSCR (0.90–1.10):** Look at interest-only loan options — they reduce the monthly PITIA, improving your DSCR ratio. A 30-year I/O term on a $300K loan at 7.5% produces a payment of $1,875 vs. $2,097 fully amortizing — that's a meaningful DSCR difference. Or, simply increase your down payment to reduce the loan amount and improve the ratio.

**Thin reserves after down payment:** Some lenders allow gift funds or business account reserves for the reserve requirement. Check lender-specific guidelines. Also consider the closing timeline — if you close at the beginning of a month, the first payment isn't due for 30–60 days, giving you time to rebuild reserves from operating income.

**Limited income history:** DSCR loans have no personal income requirement, so this isn't a DSCR-specific concern. If you're being declined on income grounds at a lender, they may not actually be a DSCR lender — verify the product type.

## Running the Numbers: A Practical Example

Say you're evaluating a $325,000 SFR in a Midwest cash-flow market:
- Estimated rent: $2,400/month
- Purchase price: $325,000
- Down payment (25%): $81,250
- Loan amount: $243,750
- Rate (7.5% 30-year fixed): ~$1,706/month P&I
- Taxes + Insurance: ~$400/month
- **Total PITIA: ~$2,106/month**
- **DSCR: $2,400 ÷ $2,106 = 1.14** ✓

At 1.14 DSCR and a 680+ credit score, this deal qualifies at the majority of non-QM DSCR lenders. The cash-on-cash return on $81,250 invested, at roughly $294/month net cash flow, is about 4.3% — modest, but this is before appreciation and principal paydown. ## Bottom Line

DSCR loan requirements in 2026 are:
- **Credit:** 620–680 minimum (640–680 for practical access)
- **Down payment:** 20–25%
- **DSCR ratio:** 1.0+ standard (some programs accept lower)
- **Property:** SFR, 2–4 unit, or STR-eligible residential property
- **Reserves:** 3–6 months PITIA after closing

The flexibility of no personal income verification makes DSCR the go-to loan for investors scaling beyond their conventional loan capacity. The variation in lender requirements makes comparison shopping before applying — not after — the move that protects your credit and gets you the best rate.
