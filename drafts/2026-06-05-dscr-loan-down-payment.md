# DSCR Loan Down Payment Requirements: What Investors Need to Know in 2026

Most DSCR loans require a **20% down payment** (80% LTV). Some lenders allow 15% down for borrowers with a 700+ FICO score on loans under $1 million. Cash-out refinances require you to maintain at least 25–30% equity. Here's what determines your exact requirement — and how to reduce it.

---

## Standard DSCR Down Payment Requirements

The baseline for residential DSCR loans is 20% down, which brings your loan-to-value ratio to 80%. This is lower than the 25% minimum required for conventional investment property loans — one reason DSCR loans have become the go-to financing tool for buy-and-hold investors.

Here's what you should actually expect across the DSCR loan market in 2026:

| Scenario | Typical Down Payment | Max LTV |
|---|---|---|
| Standard purchase (620–679 FICO) | 20–25% | 75–80% |
| Strong purchase (700+ FICO, loan ≤ $1M) | 15–20% | 80–85% |
| Cash-out refinance | 25–30% equity retained | 70–75% |
| Short-term rental (Airbnb / VRBO) | 20–25% | 75–80% |
| Multifamily (2–4 units) | 20–25% | 75–80% |
| Loan > $1.5M (jumbo DSCR) | 25–35% | 65–75% |

The "15% down" programs are real but come with conditions: a strong credit score, a loan amount under $1 million, and typically a DSCR of at least 1.20 on the subject property. If those boxes aren't checked, 20% is the floor at most reputable lenders.

## 5 Factors That Determine Your Exact Down Payment

The range above isn't arbitrary. DSCR lenders tie each tier to a specific combination of risk factors. Understanding these levers lets you structure deals — and sometimes improve your position — before you apply.

### 1. Credit Score (FICO)

Your FICO score is the single most powerful lever you have over your down payment requirement. DSCR lenders set explicit LTV caps by credit score tier:

| FICO Score | Typical Max LTV | Min Down Payment |
|---|---|---|
| 760+ | 80–85% | 15–20% |
| 720–759 | 80% | 20% |
| 680–719 | 75–80% | 20–25% |
| 640–679 | 70–75% | 25–30% |
| 620–639 | 65–70% | 30–35% |

A 40-point improvement in your credit score — say, from 670 to 710 — can shift you from a 25% down payment to 20% on the same property. On a $400,000 purchase, that's $20,000 in freed-up capital.

### 2. The Property's DSCR Ratio

DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) measures the property's rental income relative to its total mortgage payment: `DSCR = Gross Monthly Rent ÷ Monthly PITIA`.

A higher DSCR means lower risk to the lender. Most programs let you borrow more (i.e., put less down) when the ratio is stronger:

- **DSCR ≥ 1.25:** Qualifies for best-tier LTV programs
- **DSCR 1.0–1.24:** Standard 80% LTV typically available
- **DSCR 0.75–0.99:** Limited programs; expect 70–75% LTV (25–30% down)
- **DSCR < 0.75:** Most lenders decline outright [VERIFY: confirm sub-0.75 availability across major lenders in 2026]

### 3. Property Type

Single-family rentals (1-unit) get the most favorable treatment. As property type complexity increases, so does the required down payment:

- **1-unit SFR:** Lowest risk profile; maximum LTV programs apply
- **2–4 unit (small multifamily):** Most lenders treat these similarly to SFR; some require an extra 5% down
- **Short-term rentals (Airbnb/VRBO):** Lenders calculate income using AirDNA or Rentometer estimates instead of a signed lease. Good programs exist, but expect 20–25% minimum and confirm the lender explicitly offers STR DSCR [VERIFY: confirm STR DSCR availability at Kiavi, Visio, and Griffin Funding as of mid-2026]
- **Condos:** Generally treated like SFR for DSCR purposes, but warrantable condo requirements apply

### 4. Loan Size

Jumbo DSCR loans — typically above $1 million, and definitely above $2 million — carry higher down payment requirements because the secondary market for these loans is thinner:

- **< $1M:** Standard LTV programs available
- **$1M–$2M:** Expect 20–25% minimum at most lenders
- **$2M–$3M:** 25–30% typically required
- **> $3M:** Portfolio-specific; terms negotiated directly

### 5. Purchase vs. Refinance

Purchase transactions allow the highest LTV. For refinances, lenders distinguish between rate-and-term and cash-out:

- **Rate-and-term refinance:** Can maintain up to 80% LTV in most cases
- **Cash-out refinance:** Max LTV drops to 70–75%, meaning you must retain 25–30% equity after closing
- **BRRRR investors:** The cash-out refinance step is the most critical phase. If you've completed a value-add rehab and your after-repair value has increased significantly, you may be able to pull out your original investment while staying within the 75% LTV limit — but the numbers must work. ## How to Reduce Your Required Down Payment

You have more control over your down payment requirement than most investors realize:

**1. Improve your credit score before applying.** Even a 20–30 point improvement can push you into a lower down payment tier. Pay down revolving balances and avoid new credit inquiries 6 months before applying.

**2. Maximize the property's DSCR.** Negotiate a rent-ready condition or a seller rent-back to ensure strong initial rental income. A property with a 1.30 DSCR qualifies for better terms than one at 1.05.

**3. Choose the right lender for your profile.** Lenders vary significantly. One might require 25% for your credit tier while another quotes 20% with identical terms otherwise. Use the [lender finder](/lenders/finder) to compare options specific to your credit score and loan amount.

**4. Consider seller concessions for closing costs.** Seller concessions (typically 2–6% of purchase price) can cover origination fees and closing costs, reducing your total cash-to-close — though they cannot reduce the down payment itself. This preserves capital for reserves.

**5. Leverage the BRRRR stack.** If you acquire with hard money or cash, complete the rehab, and refinance via DSCR, you may recover most of your capital even with a 25% equity retention requirement — provided the ARV-to-cost math works. Model it first.

## DSCR Down Payment vs. Conventional Investment Property Loans

Many investors assume DSCR loans require more capital. In practice, they're competitive:

| | DSCR Loan | Conventional Investment |
|---|---|---|
| Min down payment | 15–20% | 15–25% |
| Income documentation | Rental income only | Full personal W-2/tax returns |
| Property count limit | None | 10 properties |
| Rate premium vs. primary | +1.5–2.5% | +0.5–1.5% |
| Close in LLC | Yes | Typically no |
| Prepayment penalty | Common (3–5 yr) | Rare |

The primary trade-off is rate: DSCR loans cost more in interest rate, but they offer flexibility that conventional loans don't — no income documentation, no property count limits, and entity ownership. For investors building a portfolio past 4–5 properties, DSCR is often the only viable path regardless of down payment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use a DSCR loan for my first investment property?**  
Yes. Most DSCR lenders have no experience requirement. You may face slightly lower max LTV (75% instead of 80%) depending on the lender, but many now offer first-timer programs at the same terms as experienced investors.

**Can I use gift funds or a HELOC for the down payment?**  
Gift funds are uncommon for investment properties but some lenders allow them from family members with proper documentation. A HELOC on your primary residence is allowed but must be disclosed — lenders will factor the payment into the debt service calculation. Seller concessions can reduce closing costs but cannot substitute for the down payment.

**What's the minimum DSCR to qualify?**  
Most lenders require a minimum DSCR of 1.00 (break-even). Some offer "no-ratio" or sub-1.0 DSCR programs (sometimes down to 0.75) at higher rates and lower LTV. A DSCR above 1.20 typically unlocks the best terms.

**Do I need reserves in addition to the down payment?**  
Yes — essentially always. Standard reserve requirements are 3–6 months of PITIA (principal, interest, taxes, insurance, and HOA). Some lenders require 12 months for higher loan amounts. Reserve funds cannot be borrowed; they must sit in a liquid, verifiable account post-closing.

**How do DSCR down payments compare for short-term rentals?**  
Most lenders accept STR income documentation (AirDNA, STR management reports) but may apply a 75% LTV cap (25% down) versus the standard 80% for long-term rentals. Confirm your target lender explicitly supports STR DSCR before proceeding.

**Can I buy with less than 20% down using DSCR?**  
A limited number of lenders offer 15% down at 85% LTV for borrowers with 700+ FICO and loan amounts under $1 million. This is less common and typically requires a clean credit profile and strong DSCR on the property.
