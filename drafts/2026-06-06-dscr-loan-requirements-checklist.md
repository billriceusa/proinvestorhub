# DSCR Loan Requirements (2026): Your Pre-Application Qualification Checklist

If your rental property generates enough monthly income to cover its mortgage payment, you can probably qualify for a DSCR loan — regardless of how your personal tax returns look. Here's exactly what lenders will check, how to calculate your number before you apply, and what to do when your deal is on the borderline.

---

## What Is a DSCR Loan (and Why Investors Use It)?

A **Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) loan** qualifies you based on the investment property's cash flow — not your W-2 income, tax returns, or personal debt-to-income ratio. Lenders divide the property's gross monthly rent by the monthly mortgage payment (principal, interest, taxes, insurance, and HOA — called PITIA) to get your DSCR.

**The formula:**

> **DSCR = Gross Monthly Rent ÷ Monthly PITIA**

A DSCR of 1.0 means the property exactly covers the debt. Most lenders want 1.10–1.25. Some programs accept 0.80 with strong compensating factors.

This structure is purpose-built for real estate investors who:
- Self-report income or run their income through an LLC
- Have significant depreciation deductions that make their taxable income look lower than their actual earnings
- Want to scale a portfolio beyond the Fannie Mae 10-property conventional limit
- Are buying under an LLC for liability protection

## The 6 Core DSCR Loan Requirements

Every lender will evaluate the same six variables. The ranges below reflect typical market standards as of mid-2026 — individual lenders vary, so use these as planning benchmarks, not guarantees.

### 1. DSCR Ratio (Most Important)

| DSCR | Typical Outcome |
|------|----------------|
| 1.25+ | Strong approval, best rate pricing |
| 1.10–1.24 | Standard approval at most lenders |
| 1.00–1.09 | Approved with compensating factors (higher credit score, larger down payment) |
| 0.80–0.99 | Limited lenders; expect higher rates and stricter terms |
| Below 0.80 | Very few programs; often requires 35%+ down or specialty portfolio lender |

**How rent is calculated:** Lenders use whichever is lower — your signed lease, or the market rent estimate from the appraisal (Form 1007 for long-term rentals). For short-term rentals (Airbnb, VRBO), lenders typically order an AirDNA or Mashvisor income report, or use 75% of the trailing 12-month gross income.

### 2. Credit Score

Most programs start at 620–640. Better credit unlocks lower rates and higher LTVs:

- **760+:** Best rate tier
- **720–759:** Standard pricing
- **680–719:** Slightly higher rate
- **640–679:** Most programs still work; expect a rate add-on of 0.25–0.50%
- **Below 640:** Very limited options; focus on improving score before applying

Unlike conventional loans, DSCR lenders weigh credit score less heavily because the property itself is the primary underwriting factor.

### 3. Down Payment / LTV

Standard minimum down payment is **20–25% for a purchase** (75–80% LTV). Cash-out refinances are typically capped at 70–75% LTV.

Some lenders offer 85% LTV on purchases for borrowers with strong credit (720+) and DSCR above 1.25. Treat this as an exception, not the norm.

### 4. Post-Close Reserves

This requirement is consistently underreported in competitor guides. After closing, lenders want to see liquid cash reserves equal to:

- **3–6 months of PITIA** for most programs
- **6–12 months** for vacation/short-term rental properties (higher income volatility)
- **Per-property** if you're financing multiple properties simultaneously

Reserves must be in a verifiable account (bank statements, brokerage). Equity in other properties typically does not count.

### 5. Property Type Eligibility

Most DSCR programs cover:
- ✅ Single-family residences (SFRs)
- ✅ 2–4 unit multifamily
- ✅ Condos (warrantable and sometimes non-warrantable)
- ✅ Short-term rentals (Airbnb/VRBO)
- ⚠️ 5+ unit multifamily (some programs, typically classified as commercial)
- ❌ Owner-occupied primary residences
- ❌ Land, agricultural, or mixed-use commercial

### 6. Entity Structure

You can close in your **personal name or under an LLC**. Most lenders handle both. If closing in an LLC, prepare to provide:

- LLC articles of organization
- Operating agreement
- Certificate of good standing (from the state — typically pulled within 90 days of closing)
- EIN verification

Note that some lenders charge a slightly higher rate (0.125–0.25%) for LLC closings due to increased documentation complexity.

## 3 Real DSCR Calculation Examples

### Example 1: Clear Pass

- **Purchase price:** $285,000
- **Down payment:** 25% ($71,250)
- **Loan amount:** $213,750 at 7.5%, 30-year fixed
- **Monthly PITIA:** $1,870 (principal + interest) + $210 (taxes) + $90 (insurance) = **$2,170**
- **Market rent:** $2,800/month

> DSCR = $2,800 ÷ $2,170 = **1.29** ✅

This deal sails through. The investor could potentially refinance to an 80% LTV product and recover $21,375 in equity to deploy on the next deal. Run your numbers in our [rental cash flow calculator](/calculators/rental-cashflow/).

---

### Example 2: Borderline — Use Interest-Only to Fix It

- **Purchase price:** $380,000
- **Down payment:** 20% ($76,000)
- **Loan amount:** $304,000 at 8.0%, 30-year amortizing
- **Monthly PITIA:** $2,232 (P&I) + $320 (taxes) + $120 (insurance) = **$2,672**
- **Market rent:** $2,850/month

> DSCR = $2,850 ÷ $2,672 = **1.07** — borderline at most lenders

**The fix:** Switch to a 30-year interest-only (I/O) product. The I/O payment drops to ~$2,027, which changes PITIA to $2,027 + $320 + $120 = **$2,467**.

> DSCR (I/O) = $2,850 ÷ $2,467 = **1.16** ✅

The I/O structure trades long-term equity buildup for near-term cash flow and deal approval. Best for investors planning a value-add exit or refinance within 5–7 years.

---

### Example 3: Fail — Recovery Strategy

- **Purchase price:** $425,000
- **Down payment:** 20% ($85,000)
- **Loan amount:** $340,000 at 8.25%, 30-year fixed
- **Monthly PITIA:** $2,552 + $380 (taxes) + $140 (insurance) = **$3,072**
- **Market rent:** $2,600/month

> DSCR = $2,600 ÷ $3,072 = **0.85** ❌

This deal fails at most standard programs. Recovery options:

1. **Increase down payment to 30%** — reduces loan to $297,500, PITIA drops to $2,750, DSCR improves to 0.95 (still borderline, but some specialty lenders will approve)
2. **Negotiate on price** — a $40,000 price reduction improves the math materially
3. **Shop portfolio/bank lenders** — some regional banks hold DSCR loans in-house with more flexible ratios; use the [lender finder](/lenders/finder/) to find options by state
4. **Wait for higher rents** — if the market rent trend supports $2,900+ within 12 months, revisit after you've improved the property

## The Hidden Requirements Most Guides Skip

### Prepayment Penalties

DSCR loans almost always include a **step-down prepayment penalty (also called a "prepay")** — a fee charged if you sell or refinance within the first 3–5 years. Common structures:

| Structure | Year 1 | Year 2 | Year 3 |
|-----------|--------|--------|--------|
| 3-2-1 | 3% of loan | 2% | 1% |
| 5-4-3-2-1 | 5% | 4% | 3% | 2% (Y4) | 1% (Y5) |

On a $300,000 loan with a 5-4-3-2-1 prepay, selling in Year 1 costs **$15,000** at closing before you see a dollar of profit. Always verify the prepay structure before signing. If you plan to flip, wholesale, or refi quickly, negotiate for a shorter prepay window — some lenders will reduce from 5 to 3 years, often for a small rate premium.

### Seasoning Requirements

If you're doing a cash-out refinance on a property you already own, most DSCR lenders require:

- **6 months of ownership seasoning** minimum
- Some require 12 months if you recently purchased at a lower price (to prevent rapid equity extraction)

### Appraisal Methodology for Rent

For vacant properties, lenders cannot use your projected rent — they use the **appraiser's market rent estimate** from Form 1007 (for SFRs) or Form 1025 (for 2–4 units). This matters because:

- Appraisers use comparable leases in the immediate area, which may be lower than active listings
- In markets with low rental comps, the appraised rent can undercut your actual lease by 10–15%
- Always request that the appraiser use recent leases (within 6 months) from comparable properties

For short-term rentals, the appraisal process is less standardized. Some lenders use the appraiser's estimate of market rent (annual lease equivalent), while others accept a third-party STR income report. Clarify which method your lender uses before ordering the appraisal.

## DSCR Loan Rates in 2026: What to Expect

DSCR rates run **0.75–1.50% higher than conventional investment property rates**, reflecting the non-QM risk premium. As of mid-2026:

- **30-year fixed:** approximately 7.25–8.50% depending on LTV, DSCR, and credit score
- **Interest-only options:** typically 0.125–0.25% higher than equivalent fully amortizing
- **5/6 or 7/6 ARMs:** often 0.50–0.75% lower than 30-year fixed during the initial period

Rate drivers in order of impact: (1) LTV, (2) DSCR ratio, (3) credit score, (4) loan amount, (5) property type, (6) prepay structure (shorter prepay = higher rate).

## DSCR vs. Conventional Investment Loans: When Each Makes Sense

| Factor | DSCR Loan | Conventional (Fannie/Freddie) |
|--------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Income docs | None (property only) | W-2, tax returns, DTI check |
| Property count | Unlimited | Up to 10 financed properties |
| LLC closing | Yes, most lenders | Typically no |
| Rate | Higher (7.25–8.50%) | Lower (6.50–7.50%) |
| Min credit | 620–640 | 620–680 |
| Prepayment penalty | Common | Rare |
| Best for | Self-employed, LLC investors, portfolio scaling | W-2 earners with low property count |

If you qualify for conventional financing and have fewer than 4 investment properties financed, conventional usually wins on rate. DSCR wins when you're self-employed, closing in an LLC, or scaling past the conventional limit.

## Your Pre-Application Checklist

Before calling a lender, run through this list:

**Property basics:**
- [ ] Calculate DSCR using current market rent (not optimistic projections)
- [ ] Confirm property type is eligible (SFR, 2–4 unit, STR, condo)
- [ ] Verify taxes and insurance estimates are current (lenders will verify)

**Financial position:**
- [ ] Pull credit score — aim for 680+ before applying
- [ ] Confirm post-close reserves: 3–6 months of PITIA in liquid accounts
- [ ] Calculate exact down payment including closing costs (typically 2–5% of loan amount)

**Entity / documentation:**
- [ ] Decide: personal name or LLC?
- [ ] If LLC: prepare articles, operating agreement, certificate of good standing, and EIN letter
- [ ] Collect entity bank statements if LLC has been operating

**Deal terms:**
- [ ] Ask every lender about prepayment penalty structure before committing
- [ ] Clarify how they calculate rent for vacant properties or STRs
- [ ] Compare 30-year fixed vs. interest-only for borderline DSCR deals

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I use a DSCR loan on a property I already own?**
Yes — DSCR cash-out refinances are common. Most lenders require 6 months of ownership seasoning and cap cash-out at 70–75% LTV.

**Do I need a tenant in place to qualify?**
No. If the property is vacant, the appraiser's market rent estimate is used in the DSCR calculation.

**Can my LLC take out a DSCR loan?**
Yes. Most DSCR lenders accommodate LLC closings. Expect to provide entity documentation (articles, operating agreement, certificate of good standing).

**What happens if my DSCR is below 1.0?**
Some specialty programs allow DSCR as low as 0.75–0.80 with a larger down payment (30–35%) and strong credit. Alternatively, increasing the down payment, negotiating a lower purchase price, or switching to an interest-only product can improve the ratio.

**Are DSCR loans available in all 50 states?**
Most lenders cover 40+ states. A handful of states (typically with stricter lending regulations) have limited availability. Use the [lender finder](/lenders/finder/) to filter by state.
