## What Is a DSCR Loan and Who Needs a DSCR Lender?

A DSCR loan qualifies based on the property's Debt Service Coverage Ratio — the monthly rental income divided by the monthly loan payment (principal + interest + taxes + insurance). A DSCR above 1.0 means the rental income covers the mortgage. Most lenders require a DSCR of 1.0–1.25 to approve the loan; no W-2, tax return, or personal income verification is required.

For a deeper breakdown of how DSCR underwriting works, see our [complete DSCR loan guide](/blog/dscr-loans-explained-complete-guide-real-estate-investors-2026).

**Who uses DSCR lenders most:**

- **W-2 investors who've maxed conventional loan limits.** Fannie Mae caps you at 10 financed properties; DSCR loans are outside that limit.
- **Self-employed investors** who write off too much income to show adequate debt-to-income on a conventional application.
- **BRRRR practitioners** who close a hard money loan on a distressed property, rehab it, rent it, and then refinance into a DSCR loan to hold long-term.
- **Portfolio scalers** who want to take title in an LLC without triggering a due-on-sale clause — most DSCR lenders lend to LLCs directly.

## The 7 Variables That Separate Good DSCR Lenders from Bad Ones

Most comparisons stop at interest rate. That's a mistake. Here is the full cost-of-capital picture you need to evaluate before applying:

### 1. Interest Rate Range

DSCR rates typically run 1.5–2.5 percentage points above comparable conventional investment property loans. In mid-2026, expect a range of **7.25–9.5% for most borrower profiles**. [VERIFY: Current DSCR rate range at publish — rates shift with Federal Reserve policy and secondary market conditions.] The exact rate is driven by your credit score, LTV, DSCR ratio, loan size, and property type.

### 2. Origination Points

Origination fees of 1–2 points are standard across DSCR lenders. On a $300,000 loan, that is $3,000–$6,000 paid at closing. Over a 3-year hold, one extra origination point represents an effective annualized rate increase of roughly 0.33%. Always compare total cost at close, not just the note rate.

### 3. Prepayment Penalty Structure

This is the most underrated variable for BRRRR investors and anyone who may sell or refinance within five years. Common structures include:

- **3-2-1 step-down** (Kiavi): 3% penalty in year 1, 2% in year 2, 1% in year 3, then none
- **5-4-3-2-1 step-down**: More punishing, common on lower-rate loans
- **Flat 3% for 3 years**: No step-down; full penalty applies anytime in the first 36 months

If you plan to refinance into a portfolio loan within two years, a 3-2-1 step-down costs roughly $6,000 on a $300,000 loan. A five-year step-down on the same loan can cost $15,000 or more. This question must be answered before you sign.

### 4. Minimum DSCR Ratio

Most lenders require 1.0–1.25. Griffin Funding will go to 0.75 for strong-credit borrowers, while Lima One Capital requires 1.5 or higher. The lender's minimum determines which properties they will fund. A property at 1.1 DSCR is approved at some lenders and a hard decline at others.

### 5. Credit Score Requirements

Requirements vary widely across the market. Griffin Funding accepts 580+ FICO (with a higher required down payment below 640); most lenders require 620–660 as the floor, with best pricing beginning at 700+. Know your score before shopping — it determines which lenders you can approach realistically and at what pricing tier.

### 6. Property Type Eligibility

Most DSCR lenders focus exclusively on 1–4 unit residential. Coverage for five-plus unit properties, non-warrantable condos, short-term rentals, and mixed-use varies significantly by lender. Angel Oak has particularly strong short-term rental underwriting using AirDNA income data. Kiavi is limited to 1–4 unit residential. For condos, always ask whether the lender accepts non-warrantable projects.

### 7. Geographic Coverage

Business-purpose loans — which DSCR loans are — are largely exempt from state mortgage licensing requirements, but some lenders voluntarily choose not to lend in certain states due to legal or operational reasons. LendingOne, for example, does not lend in Alaska, Nevada, North Dakota, or South Dakota. Always confirm your target state before investing time in an application.

## 8 Questions to Ask Every DSCR Lender Before Applying

This framework does not appear in any of the top-10 results for this keyword — because they were all written by lenders who cannot credibly answer these questions about themselves. Use all eight on every initial call.

**1. "What is your minimum DSCR, and how do you calculate it?"**
The calculation method matters more than the stated minimum. Some lenders use PITIA (principal + interest + taxes + insurance + association dues); others calculate DSCR on PI only. A DSCR of 1.0 on PITIA is very different from 1.0 on PI. Confirm which formula applies before you run your numbers.

**2. "What rental income sources do you accept for short-term rental properties?"**
If you are buying an Airbnb or VRBO property, ask explicitly: Do they accept AirDNA projections, a 1007 schedule (market rent appraisal), or only a signed long-term lease? Many lenders decline STR properties entirely. Wasting three weeks in underwriting on a deal the lender was never going to approve is an avoidable error.

**3. "What is the exact prepayment penalty, and are there exceptions for a property sale vs. a refinance?"**
Get this in writing before you are in underwriting. Some lenders distinguish between a payoff triggered by a sale versus a refinance — and some will waive the penalty under specific conditions. The standard term sheet often buries this in the note.

**4. "Do you offer relationship pricing for repeat borrowers?"**
Even on your first transaction, asking this question signals that you are a portfolio investor — and it sometimes triggers a pricing discussion that would not have happened otherwise. LendingOne and RCN Capital have explicit volume discount programs.

**5. "What are your typical closing timeline milestones?"**
"21 days" is a marketing claim, not a process commitment. Ask: How many business days from application to conditional approval? How many from conditional approval to clear-to-close? Who is responsible for the bottleneck if it slips? The answers reveal whether the lender's operations match their marketing.

**6. "What are the most common reasons you decline DSCR applications?"**
This is the question no one on the borrower side asks. The answer reveals the lender's actual hard stops: property condition requirements, market concentration limits, LLC structure issues, title seasoning requirements, and the property types they decline without advertising it. A 10-minute conversation on this can save you weeks.

**7. "Do you hold the loan on your balance sheet or sell it into the secondary market?"**
Portfolio lenders who hold their own paper have more flexibility on edge cases because they set their own guidelines. Lenders who originate-and-sell are constrained by secondary market investor guidelines that may change without notice. Your loan may be sold to a servicer with substantially different policies than the originator.

**8. "Can you walk me through an itemized closing cost estimate on a $350K purchase at 75% LTV?"**
Force full transparency: origination fee, underwriting fee, appraisal, title, recording, and any additional lender fees. Compare this itemized estimate across at least three lenders before choosing. The gap between the cheapest and most expensive total cost-of-close on a $350K loan can easily exceed $4,000.

## DSCR Lender Red Flags to Walk Away From

Lender-written roundups cannot include this section — it would be self-incriminating. Here is what independent due diligence looks like:

**Bait-and-switch interest rates.** The rate in the ad is not the rate you close at unless your credit score, LTV, and DSCR are at the lender's best-case scenario. If you are not receiving a locked Loan Estimate within the first week of underwriting, the quoted rate is marketing, not a commitment.

**Shifting or multiplying underwriting conditions.** A conditional approval with vague or repeatedly expanding conditions is a sign that the underwriter is improvising or that the loan is being shopped to secondary market investors who have not committed to buy it.

**Undisclosed broker relationships.** Some entities marketing themselves as "lenders" are mortgage brokers. There is nothing wrong with brokers — they can often find better pricing than going direct — but you deserve to know who the actual capital source is and whether there is a broker fee layered on top of the origination fee.

**Pressure to waive the property inspection.** Any lender pushing you to skip property condition review on a DSCR refinance is protecting their own closing timeline. They will not be living with the deferred maintenance risk. You will.

**Appraisal steering.** If the lender insists on a specific appraiser outside a normal AMC rotation, ask why. Appraisal inflation in DSCR underwriting creates a risk that the stated value — and therefore the DSCR calculation — does not reflect reality when the market moves.

**"No prepayment penalty" with yield maintenance fine print.** Some lenders advertise no prepayment penalty during an initial period, then embed yield maintenance or defeasance clauses in the note that apply under specific payoff scenarios. Read the note, not the marketing sheet.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What DSCR do I need to qualify?**
Most lenders require a minimum of 1.0; the best pricing typically requires 1.25. Griffin Funding accepts ratios as low as 0.75 for borrowers with strong credit. Use our [lender finder](/lenders/finder) to filter by the DSCR your property actually achieves.

**Can I take out a DSCR loan in an LLC?**
Yes — this is one of DSCR's structural advantages over conventional investment financing. Most DSCR lenders lend directly to LLCs, S-corps, and trusts. Almost all will require a personal guarantee from the entity's principal.

**How long does a DSCR loan take to close?**
Fourteen to thirty days for most lenders in standard market conditions. Kiavi's bridge-to-DSCR product and repeat-borrower programs can close faster. Complex properties — short-term rentals, non-warrantable condos, loans above $1M — typically add one to two weeks.

**Are DSCR loans available for multifamily (5+ units)?**
The market for 5+ unit DSCR loans is much thinner than for 1–4 unit residential. Most national DSCR lenders stop at four units. For properties above that threshold, you are entering commercial DSCR territory with different underwriting standards and a smaller lender pool. **What is the minimum loan amount for a DSCR loan?**
