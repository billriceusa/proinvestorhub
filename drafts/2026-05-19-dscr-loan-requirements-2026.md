# DSCR Loan Requirements in 2026: The Investor's Qualification Checklist

To qualify for a DSCR loan in 2026, you need a minimum debt service coverage ratio of 1.0–1.25, a credit score of at least 620–640, a 20–25% down payment, and six months of cash reserves remaining after closing. Unlike conventional mortgages, there are no W-2s, tax returns, or debt-to-income calculations — lenders focus entirely on whether the property's rental income covers the mortgage payment. This guide walks through every requirement with worked examples so you can screen deals before talking to a single lender.

---

## What Is a DSCR Loan and Why Investors Use It

A DSCR loan (Debt Service Coverage Ratio loan) is a non-QM investment property loan that underwrites based on property cash flow rather than the borrower's personal income. If the rent covers the mortgage, you can qualify — regardless of how your tax returns look.

That last point matters. Most active real estate investors use aggressive depreciation and expense write-offs that make their taxable income look far lower than their actual earnings. A self-employed investor with $200K in gross rental income might show $40K on their tax return after deductions. Conventional lenders see the $40K and decline. A DSCR lender sees the properties and approves.

**When DSCR makes more sense than conventional financing:**
- You've hit the Fannie Mae 10-financed-property limit
- Your W-2 income is low relative to your actual cash flow
- You want to close in an LLC for asset protection
- You're scaling quickly and want streamlined underwriting
- You're buying a short-term rental (Airbnb/VRBO) with no long-term lease

## The 5 Core DSCR Loan Requirements

### 1. Minimum DSCR Ratio

The DSCR ratio is the central underwriting metric. The formula:

**DSCR = Gross Monthly Rental Income ÷ Monthly PITIA**

PITIA = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + HOA (if any)

Lenders price loans on three tiers:

| DSCR Ratio | What It Means | Typical Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| 1.25 or higher | Rent covers mortgage by 25%+ | Best rates, highest LTV, easiest approval |
| 1.00 – 1.24 | Property breaks even to modestly cash-flows | Standard approval, slightly higher rate |
| Below 1.00 | Rent doesn't fully cover payment | "No-ratio" loan — requires 30–35% down, higher rate |

**Worked example:** A $350,000 SFR rents for $2,400/month. With 20% down ($70,000), a 7.5% rate on a 30-year loan, and taxes/insurance of $450/month, the PITIA is roughly $2,610/month.

DSCR = $2,400 ÷ $2,610 = **0.92** — below the 1.0 threshold.

To get to 1.0, you'd need to either put more down (reducing the payment) or find a property with higher rent. Use the [Rental Cash Flow Calculator](/calculators/rental-cashflow) to stress-test any deal before submitting an application.

**Note on short-term rentals:** If you're financing an Airbnb or VRBO, lenders accept projected income from an appraisal or AirDNA market report in lieu of a lease. Most lenders apply a 10–20% "haircut" to projected STR income to account for vacancy and seasonality. Run your numbers with that reduction applied — if the deal still hits 1.0, you're likely approvable. [VERIFY: confirm current AirDNA acceptance rate among major DSCR lenders in 2026]

### 2. Credit Score Requirements

Because DSCR lenders skip income verification, your credit history becomes the primary signal of repayment reliability. Minimum scores exist, but the tier you're in determines your rate and maximum LTV:

| Credit Score | Impact |
|---|---|
| 740+ | Maximum LTV (up to 80%), lowest available rates |
| 700–739 | Standard rates, 75–80% LTV |
| 660–699 | Modestly higher rate, 70–75% LTV |
| 620–659 | Minimum qualifying range for most lenders; limited LTV (65–70%), rate 1–2% above top tier |

**The bankruptcy question:** DSCR loans typically require only a 3–4 year waiting period after a bankruptcy or foreclosure — significantly shorter than the 7-year wait for conventional loans. If you're rebuilding after a rough market cycle, this matters.

**Practical tip:** If your score is in the 660–679 range, paying down revolving credit card balances can quickly move the needle. Even a 20-point improvement can save you 0.25–0.5% on the rate and unlock higher LTV — which directly affects how much cash you need to close.

---

### 3. Down Payment and LTV Requirements

DSCR loans are investor products, and lenders expect meaningful equity in the deal:

- **Purchase:** 20% down (80% LTV) is the standard minimum. At 25% down (75% LTV), you often unlock slightly better pricing.
- **Cash-out refinance:** Maximum 70–75% LTV. Lenders are more conservative when you're pulling equity out.
- **Rate/term refinance:** Up to 80% LTV in most programs.

**Can you use equity from another property for the down payment?** Yes — and this is one of the most powerful scaling strategies available. Do a DSCR cash-out refinance on an existing property to pull equity, then use those funds as the down payment on the next acquisition. Your capital stays in motion rather than sitting idle.

### 4. Cash Reserve Requirements

This is the requirement that most competitor articles skip, and it's the one that catches investors off guard at the closing table.

**Standard reserve requirement:** 6 months of PITIA payments in liquid assets, *remaining after closing*.

**Concrete example:** If your PITIA on the new property is $2,200/month, you need $13,200 sitting in accessible accounts *after* you've paid the down payment and closing costs. If you drain your savings to cover the down payment, you'll fail the reserve check even if your credit and DSCR are perfect.

**Acceptable reserve sources:**
- Personal or business bank accounts (100% of balance counts)
- Brokerage accounts (typically 70% of vested value)
- 401(k) / IRA (typically 70% of vested value, not all lenders accept)
- Gift funds from family (not accepted by all lenders — confirm upfront)

**Some lenders require reserves for each property you own** across your entire portfolio, not just the new acquisition. If you own four rentals and are adding a fifth, that reserve requirement can be substantial. [VERIFY: confirm whether portfolio-wide reserve stacking is standard or lender-specific in 2026]

### 5. Property Type Eligibility

Most DSCR programs accept:
- Single-family homes (SFR) — the most common and easiest to underwrite
- 2–4 unit multifamily (duplex, triplex, fourplex)
- Condos and townhomes (warrantability requirements may apply)
- Short-term rentals (Airbnb/VRBO) — increasingly standard in 2026

**What typically doesn't qualify for residential DSCR:**
- 5+ unit multifamily (falls under commercial lending)
- Mixed-use properties (residential + commercial space)
- Land and raw development projects
- Properties with active code violations

**2026 update on short-term rentals:** Two years ago, most DSCR lenders avoided STR financing. Today it's a standard product category. Lenders now accept AirDNA projections or Form 1007 Rent Schedules (marked for STR use) from licensed appraisers. If the appraiser's projected rent — after the lender's haircut — still produces a DSCR above 1.0, the loan is underwritable.

## How to Calculate Your DSCR Before Applying

The most common reason investors get surprised by a DSCR denial is applying before doing the math themselves. Here's the full calculation:

**Step 1 — Estimate gross monthly rent.** Use actual lease or appraiser's market rent estimate (Form 1007). For STRs, use AirDNA projected income × 0.85 (conservative haircut).

**Step 2 — Calculate your PITIA.** Use the [Mortgage Calculator](/calculators/mortgage) to get the P&I payment on your loan amount, then add:
- Estimated property taxes (annual taxes ÷ 12)
- Landlord insurance premium (annual premium ÷ 12)
- HOA fees (if applicable)

**Step 3 — Divide.** Gross rent ÷ PITIA = your DSCR.

**Full example:**
- Property: $380,000 purchase price
- Down payment: 20% = $76,000
- Loan amount: $304,000
- Rate: 7.75% (30-year fixed)
- P&I payment: ~$2,177/month
- Taxes: $4,800/year → $400/month
- Insurance: $1,800/year → $150/month
- PITIA total: **$2,727/month**
- Market rent: **$2,800/month**
- **DSCR = $2,800 ÷ $2,727 = 1.03** — qualifies, though tightly. Best to increase rent or put more down.

## DSCR vs. Conventional vs. Hard Money: Quick Comparison

| Factor | DSCR Loan | Conventional Investment | Hard Money |
|---|---|---|---|
| Income verification | None | W-2 / tax returns required | None |
| Credit score minimum | 620–640 | 620–680 | 550–600 (asset-based) |
| Down payment | 20–25% | 15–25% | 10–25% |
| Rate (2026 approx.) | 7–9% | 6–8% | 10–13% |
| Max properties | Unlimited | 10 (Fannie Mae) | Unlimited |
| Close in LLC | Yes | No | Yes |
| Best for | Scaling rental portfolio | 1–10 properties, W-2 income | Fix-and-flip, bridge |

**When conventional wins:** If you have a strong W-2, fewer than 10 financed properties, and want the lowest possible rate, conventional investment property loans typically beat DSCR by 1–1.5% in rate. ## DSCR Loans in the BRRRR Strategy

BRRRR — Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat — relies on DSCR financing for the Refinance step. Here's how it connects:

1. **Buy** a distressed property with hard money or cash.
2. **Rehab** to force appreciation and increase rental value.
3. **Rent** to a qualified tenant (or furnish for STR).
4. **Refinance** with a DSCR loan — the improved rent and ARV let you pull out your rehab capital.
5. **Repeat** with the returned capital.

The DSCR loan is what makes BRRRR scalable. Because it underwrites on rental income rather than your personal income, you can repeat this cycle without conventional loan limits or income documentation slowing you down. ## DSCR Loan Documentation Checklist

The appeal of DSCR loans is the short documentation list. What you'll actually need:

- **Standard loan application (Form 1003)**
- **2 months of bank statements** — to verify down payment source and cash reserves
- **Property insurance binder** — lender needs this before closing; get a quote early
- **LLC documents** (if closing in entity) — Articles of Organization, Operating Agreement, EIN letter
- **Appraisal with Form 1007 Rent Schedule** — the lender orders this, but the appraiser's rent determination controls your DSCR. Consider meeting the appraiser at the property with rent comps from Rentometer, Zillow, or local property management data to support a higher market rent figure
- **Existing lease** (if tenant is in place) — a signed lease with a creditworthy tenant at or above market rent strengthens your application considerably

**What you don't need:** tax returns, W-2s, pay stubs, employment verification, or personal income statements.

## 5 Ways to Improve Your DSCR Before Applying

If your numbers come in below 1.0 or 1.25, here are the levers you can pull:

**1. Increase the rent.** Minor unit improvements (new appliances, fresh paint, in-unit laundry) can raise market rent by $100–$300/month, which often moves a borderline DSCR into approvable territory.

**2. Put more down.** A larger down payment reduces the loan balance and monthly P&I. Going from 20% to 25% down can add 0.05–0.15 to your DSCR ratio depending on the deal.

**3. Buy down the rate with points.** Paying 1–2 points upfront to lower the interest rate reduces your P&I payment, which raises DSCR. This is particularly effective if you plan to hold long-term.

**4. Choose an interest-only structure.** Interest-only DSCR loans dramatically reduce the monthly payment (you're not paying principal), which boosts DSCR. The trade-off: you're not building equity through amortization. Best suited for investors focused on cash flow over equity paydown.

**5. Find a better property.** The simplest solution. If a deal's DSCR is 0.85 and would require heroic rent improvements to qualify, the next deal might start at 1.15. Use the [Rental Cash Flow Calculator](/calculators/rental-cashflow) to pre-screen properties before making offers.

## How to Find the Right DSCR Lender

DSCR loans are not standardized the way conventional mortgages are. Two lenders quoting the same borrower, same property, same loan size can differ by 1–1.5% in rate. That spread on a $300,000 loan amounts to roughly $2,500–$3,000 per year in additional interest.

**What to compare across lenders:**
- Minimum DSCR ratio accepted (1.0 vs. 1.25 vs. 0.75 "no-ratio" programs)
- Rate pricing by credit score tier
- Prepayment penalty structure (a 5-year step-down penalty locks you in — know it before you sign)
- STR eligibility and how they handle AirDNA projections
- LLC closing acceptance
- Reserve requirements (does the lender stack reserves across your portfolio?)
- Minimum and maximum loan amounts

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does a DSCR loan affect my personal debt-to-income ratio?**
Typically no. Because DSCR loans are often closed in an LLC or underwritten on property performance rather than personal income, many lenders don't report them to personal credit in a way that impacts your DTI for other financing. Confirm with your specific lender, as reporting practices vary.

**Can I use a DSCR loan for my first investment property?**
Yes — most DSCR programs accept first-time investors. Some lenders offer better terms (higher LTV, lower rate) to investors with a track record of owning at least one other property, but having no prior rentals doesn't automatically disqualify you.

**What's the maximum loan amount for DSCR loans?**
Most programs go up to $3–5 million for single properties. Some portfolio lenders extend higher. Loan minimums are typically $100,000–$150,000, which effectively excludes very low-price-point markets.

**Is a prepayment penalty common on DSCR loans?**
Yes, and it's one of the most important terms to negotiate. A typical structure is a 5-year step-down (5% penalty in year 1, declining by 1% per year). If you might sell or refinance within 3–5 years, price this into your exit math — it can cost $10,000–$30,000 on a $300,000 loan.

**Can I get a DSCR loan on a property I already own?**
Yes — a DSCR cash-out refinance lets you extract equity from a property you own free-and-clear or with significant equity. The DSCR calculation still applies: the post-refinance payment must be supported by the property's rental income.
